# The impact of online consumer reviews on purchase intentions for electronic products

Pooja Katyal<sup>1,2</sup>  · Reetika Sehgal<sup>3</sup>  · Anand Kumar Gupta<sup>1</sup>

Received: 22 November 2024 / Accepted: 27 May 2025 / Published online: 25 June 2025 © The Author(s) under exclusive licence to The Society for Reliability Engineering, Quality and Operations Management (SREQOM), India and The Division of Operation and Maintenance. Lulea University of Technology. Sweden 2025

Abstract In today’s digital era, online consumer reviews (OCRs) signifcantly infuence purchasing decisions, especially for electronic products, which require careful evaluation due to their technical features, cost, and durability. This study examines the impact of OCRs on consumer purchase intentions for electronic products on e-commerce platforms. Using a quantitative research approach, data was collected from 227 respondents in India through a fve-point Likertscale questionnaire, which included open-ended questions on three key factors: online review credibility, reviewer credibility, and platform credibility. Cronbach’s Alpha was used to assess data reliability, and factor analysis was performed to identify the key determinants afecting purchase intentions. The fndings reveal seven critical components of OCRs that shape consumer purchase intentions: website experience, review reliability, risk reduction, product evaluation, purchase motivation, trust enhancement, and ease of purchase. The study also discusses the Elaboration Likelihood Model, which supports the role of OCRs in consumer decision-making. These insights contribute to existing research while ofering practical implications for e-commerce managers seeking to improve their online reputation and build consumer trust. The study concludes with its limitations and suggestions for future research.

Keywords Electronic word of mouth (eWOM) · Online consumer reviews (OCRs) · e-commerce websites · Electronic products · Exploratory factor analysis (EFA)

## 1 Introduction

Word of mouth (WOM) is interpersonal communication between consumers regarding the consumption of a brand, product, or service which changes the consumers’ behavior and attitude (Godes & Mayzlin 2004). There is a strong inclination toward the suggestions of relatives and friends and people are easily persuaded by them (Sundaram et al., 1998). Marketers fnd it lucrative as it is a powerful and effective way of advertising and adopt word-of-mouth channels to reach the audience through this innovative way of communication (Pham 2022). It is believed that in the middle of the 1990s, electronic word of mouth, or eWOM, replaced traditional word of mouth when the Internet became a vital part of our lives and connected us with people worldwide (Verma & Yadav 2021). eWOM refers to the process of sharing information about a product or service to a large audience via various forms such as posts by infuencers on social media, online consumer reviews on e-commerce websites, discussions on online forums, emails to consumers, and blogs by people (Lim et al. 2022). As AI and machine learning continue to evolve, virtual infuencers will likely become more efective in generating eWOM (Al Masri et al. 2023). Therefore, the availability of the Internet and diferent online platforms have widened the choice of obtaining information about products through electronic word of mouth (Akdim 2021).

With the advent of online shopping, e-commerce has witnessed tremendous growth, revolutionizing the way consumers purchase products and services (Kim 2021). With the convenience and vast product information availability on online platforms, consumers increasingly rely on electronic channels to make informed purchasing decisions (Farki and Baihaqi 2016). Among the numerous factors impacting consumer behavior in the digital marketplace, electronic wordof-mouth (eWOM) has been a signifcant driver of consumer purchase intention (Lin et al. 2011).

eWOM refers to the sharing of consumer opinions, experiences, and recommendations about products or services through various online platforms, such as review websites, social media, and online discussion forums (Hennig-Thurau et al. 2004; Ali et al., 2019; Román et al. 2023). In the digital age, electronic word of mouth has transformed into a powerful tool that shapes consumers’ perceptions, attitudes, and ultimately, their purchase intentions (Baber et al. 2016; Thomas et al., 2019). In the case of electronic devices, which are commonly purchased online due to their wide availability and reasonable pricing, the impact of eWOM becomes even more crucial (Saragih & Widayanti 2019). Yaylc & Bayram (2012) conducted research on examining the efects of online consumer reviews on purchase decisions for electronic products and found that review quantity, characteristics of review i.e., consistency and recency of reviews, reviewers’ rating of usefulness and reliability, popularity, and internationality of website are important factors in the purchasing process.

In light of Online Consumer Reviews (OCRs), the Elaboration Likelihood Model (ELM) can be relevant in understanding how consumers process and respond to reviews and ratings when making purchase decisions. One of the most well-known theories in the area of persuasion and communication is the Elaboration Likelihood Model (ELM) which discusses the infuence of persuasive messages and the role of cognitive processing in attitude change (Hooper 2019). The ELM was developed by Richard E. Petty and John T. Cacioppo in the late 1970s (Filieri, 2014). It posits that people can be persuaded through two diferent routes, depending on their level of motivation and capacity for information processing, which are central processing and peripheral processing. (Chen 2017; Kumar et al. 2023). Individuals with the motivation and cognitive resources to carefully evaluate a persuasive message engage in central processing (Petty & Briñol 2008). In this route, people critically analyze the message, consider the arguments, and are more likely to change their attitudes if the arguments are compelling (Chou et al. 2022). When individuals lack the motivation or cognitive resources for central processing, they may rely on peripheral cues or heuristics to make judgments (Petty & Briñol 2008; Chou et al. 2022). These cues can include superfcial factors like the reviewer’s attractiveness, source credibility, or emotional appeals. In this route, attitudes may change without much deep cognitive processing (Chen 2017).

The signifcance of the present study lies in the growing importance of online consumer reviews as a source of information and decision-making infuence for consumers. While traditional marketing strategies still play a role, the power of eWOM cannot be underestimated. Therefore, understanding the factors that drive consumers to trust and act upon online consumer reviews is vital for businesses seeking to enhance their online presence and infuence consumer behavior. The study intends to shed light on the dynamic relationship between eWOM and consumer behavior, ofering practical implications for businesses operating in the ever-evolving digital marketplace.

Moreover, there is not much attention given to electronic products in the early work conducted to investigate the infuence of OCRs on consumers’ intent to buy. The current study attempts to examine the impact of OCRs on consumer purchase intention, explicitly focusing on electronic products on e-commerce websites in tier-2 cities in India. The study attempts to fll this research gap and strives to provide valuable insights for marketers, e-commerce platforms, and consumers alike by investigating the factors that shape consumers’ responses to OCRs.

To accomplish the objective the research has employed quantitative methods. The study includes a thorough review of existing literature, an analysis of real-life consumer reviews, and surveys conducted among potential consumers of electronic products on e-commerce websites. The following research question is being attempted to be answered by the current study:

RQ1: What is the impact of review credibility factors in online consumer reviews on consumers’ purchase intention when buying electronic products on an e-commerce platform?

RQ2: What is the impact of source credibility factors in online consumer reviews on consumers’ purchase intention when buying electronic products on an e-commerce platform?

RQ3: What is the impact of website credibility factors in online consumer reviews on consumers’ purchase intention when buying electronic products on an e-commerce platform?

## 3 Theoretical framework of OCRs

## 3.1 OCRs on e‑commerce websites

The evolution of the internet has led to the rise of electronic word of mouth (eWOM), an interactive medium that enables consumers to share experiences and influence purchasing decisions (Saleem & Ellahi 2017). Consumers rely on user-generated content across various platforms, including shopping websites, social media, and discussion forums, to assess products before making purchases (Hu et al. 2012; Zhang et al. 2021). Research indicates that eWOM on e-commerce websites has a stronger impact on consumer purchase intentions than social media recommendations (Erkan & Evans 2016; Abedin et al. 2021).

Online consumer reviews (OCRs), a subset of eWOM, are customer-generated evaluations posted on e-commerce and third-party websites (Farki & Baihaqi 2016). These reviews provide positive or negative insights into products or services, helping potential buyers mitigate risks and ensure quality (Stauss 2000; Masud & Hassan 2020). Consumers participate in OCRs for various reasons, including assisting others, self-enhancement, and social or economic benefits (Hennig-Thurau et al. 2004; Chai et al. 2023). Despite being posted by anonymous individuals, OCRs are often perceived as more reliable than expert or seller-generated reviews (Bickart & Schindler 2001; Park et al. 2007).

The credibility and influence of OCRs depend on several attributes, including review quantity, quality, length, timeliness, valence, and rating (Chevalier & Mayzlin 2006; Guo & Zhou 2017; Rangsang & Millayani 2021; Wang et  al. 2022; Kargozari et  al. 2023). Consumers tend to perceive products more favorably when reviews are predominantly positive, while negative reviews deter potential buyers (Park & Lee 2008; Ruytenbeek & Decock 2024). Additionally, a higher number of reviews enhances a product’s perceived popularity, further influencing purchasing behavior (Yaylı & Bayram 2012).

The presence of fake or misleading reviews is a significant concern in e-commerce, as they can influence consumer perceptions and purchase decisions (Kim et al. 2023). Fake reviews, often generated by bots, paid reviewers, or manipulated by sellers, can distort the credibility of online reviews and mislead consumers into making suboptimal purchasing choices (Luca & Zervas 2016). Studies have shown that consumers tend to rely on various heuristics, such as reviewer credibility, review consistency, and overall rating distribution, to assess the authenticity of online reviews (Filieri 2016; Chevalier & Mayzlin 2006).

To address this, our study acknowledges the potential impact of fake reviews on consumer trust and purchase behavior. While our primary focus is on the credibility factors influencing purchase intentions, we recognize the need for further research on how consumers detect and respond to fake reviews. Future studies could explore the role of AI-driven detection mechanisms, consumer awareness strategies, and platform policies in mitigating the effects of fraudulent reviews. By integrating such insights, a more comprehensive understanding of online consumer trust dynamics can be developed.

## 3.2 Elaboration likelihood model

One of the prominent theories that helps to understand the psychological process of consumers is the Elaboration Likelihood Model. ELM helps to know how online reviews persuade consumers to change their attitudes (Petty and Briñol 2008). In the past, researchers have discussed ELM theory to understand the process of persuasion and change in the attitude of the consumers which consequently infuences the purchase intention of consumers (Cheung et al. 2012). ELM theory discusses the information processing of consumers via two cues- peripheral and central cues (Petty & Wegener, 1999). Depending on the motivation and ability to process information, consumers may engage in either central or peripheral processing when evaluating reviews (Kumar et al. 2023). In the Elaboration Likelihood Model (ELM), review credibility can function as either a central or peripheral cue, depending on the consumer’s level of involvement. When consumers are highly involved (e.g., making an expensive or important purchase), they process information deeply. In this case, review credibility is a central cue, as consumers carefully assess argument quality, accuracy, completeness, and clarity (Filieri & McLeay 2014). They critically evaluate whether the review is well-supported, logical, and informative. When consumers have low involvement (e.g., routine or low-cost purchases), they rely on heuristics. Here, review credibility can act as a peripheral cue, meaning consumers may judge credibility based on surface-level factors such as review volume, star ratings, or the presence of images/videos (Iqbal et al. 2022). Rather than analyzing content deeply, they may use credibility signals like a high number of positive reviews or the presence of verifed buyers.

## 3.3 Purchase intention towards electronic products

Online consumer reviews (OCRs) have a stronger infuence on purchase intention than traditional word-of-mouth communication (Kim 2021; Pham et al. 2023). In this study, purchase intention is the dependent variable, refecting consumers’ willingness and inclination to buy a product or service. Purchase intention is infuenced by trust, which has been identifed as a key predictor of purchase decisions (McKnight & Chervany 2001).

Consumer purchase intentions vary across product categories (Pham 2022). While price sensitivity afects everyday purchases, quality plays a more signifcant role in luxury goods and electronics (Sehgal et al. 2023). Although substantial research has explored purchase intention in sectors like fashion (Kim & Kim 2004; Shin et al. 2023), hospitality (Sparks & Browning 2011), and healthcare (Tajuddin et al. 2020), limited studies focus on how online reviews impact consumer decisions for electronic products.

Given the rapid technological advancements and competitive pricing in the electronics industry, consumers actively seek information before purchasing. Traditionally, word of-mouth from family and friends influenced decisions (Kushwaha 2015; Khanna et al. 2023), but studies indicate a growing preference for OCRs as a source of frsthand product information (Cheong et al. 2020). Surveys reveal that 80% of consumers research electronic brands online before purchasing (Opinion Research Corporation 2022), reinforcing the importance of OCRs in shaping buying behavior.

Electronic products, including home appliances, mobile devices, and computing accessories, have become integral to modern life (Margaret 2013). Online reviews provide insights into features, performance, and durability, helping consumers make informed decisions (Yaylı & Bayram 2012; Cheong et al. 2020). Understanding consumer behavior in this sector enables businesses to refne their marketing strategies and enhance customer engagement.

## 3.4 Components of OCRs afecting consumer purchase intention

In this study, online consumer reviews (OCRs) are analyzed through three key dimensions: review credibility, source credibility, and website credibility. Purchase intention serves as the dependent variable, three credibility factors act as independent variables while consumer trust as a mediating variable. The study’s novel contribution lies in integrating these components into a new research framework to assess their combined infuence on consumer purchase intention. A detailed discussion of these OCR factors and their infuence on purchase intention is as follows:

## 3.4.1 Review credibility and purchase intention

Review credibility signifcantly infuences consumer purchase intentions, as consumers rely on online consumer reviews (OCRs) to assess product quality and reduce uncertainty (Liu 2006; Yen et al. 2011). Among key attributes of OCRs, review valence—whether positive, negative, or neutral—shapes consumer attitudes (Chevalier & Mayzlin 2006). While negative reviews tend to have a stronger impact than positive ones (Lee et al. 2008), a high volume of positive reviews can ofset negative perceptions and improve overall purchase likelihood (Purnawirawan et al. 2012).

Review volume is another crucial determinant of purchase intention, as products with a higher number of reviews appear more popular and attract more buyers (Duan et al. 2008; Yaylı & Bayram 2012). Consumer engagement varies based on involvement level—low-involvement consumers prioritize review quantity, whereas high-involvement consumers focus on quality (Park et al. 2007). Review length also contributes to credibility by offering detailed insights.

leading to increased consumer confdence (Mudambi & Schuf 2010; Pan & Zhang 2011).

The perceived quality of a review—determined by accuracy, completeness, and clarity—plays a critical role in its persuasiveness (Filieri & McLeay 2014). Well-structured arguments enhance credibility, making consumers more likely to trust the information (Park & Lee 2009). Furthermore, review helpfulness, often indicated by user votes, directly infuences consumer purchase decisions (Guo & Zhou 2017; Lee & Choeh 2020).

Temporal factors also afect credibility; recent reviews carry more weight than older ones, as they refect current product performance and market conditions (Sa’ait et al., 2016; Cheong et al. 2020). While star ratings provide a quick assessment of a product, research suggests they have a limited direct efect on purchase intention compared to detailed textual reviews (Alzate et al. 2021). On the other hand, multimedia-enhanced reviews (e.g., images and videos) strengthen trust and reduce perceived risk (Iqbal et al. 2022). Additionally, consistency among reviews enhances credibility, as consumers tend to trust products with aligned opinions rather than conficting evaluations (Baek et al. 2012; Tran et al. 2022).

Hypothesis 1 (H1): Review credibility factors of online consumer reviews have a signifcant impact on consumers purchase intention.

## 3.4.2 Source credibility and purchase intention

The credibility of the reviewer plays a pivotal role in shaping consumer trust and purchase decisions, as the source of information infuences perceived reliability (Rani et al. 2022). Consumers assess source credibility based on key attributes, including the reviewer’s expertise, demographic details, writing style, and perceived similarity with potential buyers (Baber et al. 2016; Liu & Park 2015; Sohaib et al. 2020).

Reviewers who disclose real names and demographic details (e.g., age, location) may appear more trustworthy, but studies indicate that such disclosures have only a minimal efect on purchase intention compared to other credibility factors (Chakraborty & Bhat 2017; Yaylı & Bayram 2012). Instead, writing style and emotional tone strongly impact review persuasiveness, as consumers resonate more with well-articulated and emotionally engaging reviews (Rani & Shivaprasad 2021).

Among source credibility factors, reviewer expertise— defned by product knowledge and the ability to provide accurate, detailed, and technical information—has the strongest infuence on purchase decisions (Guo & Zhou 2017; Hu et al., 2021). Experts’ reviews are perceived as more informative and reliable, making consumers more likely to trust their recommendations over general user feedback (Hussain et al. 2020).

Hypothesis 2 (H2): Source credibility factors of online consumer reviews have a signifcant impact on consumers purchase intention.

## 3.4.3 Website credibility and purchase intention

Advancements in technology have transformed consumer communication, enabling easy access to online platforms like e-commerce websites, discussion forums, and social media (Akdim 2021). Online consumer reviews (OCRs) on e-commerce sites serve as a key source of frsthand product information (Román et al. 2023; Lin et al. 2011).

Key factors contributing to website credibility include stability, reliability, accessibility, and platform design (Tandon et al. 2020). A well-structured and user-friendly website enhances consumer confidence, while frequent crashes or poor navigation can undermine trust (Masud & Hassan 2020). Furthermore, active website engagement, such as responding to customer queries and addressing negative reviews, reinforces credibility and builds consumer trust (Sharma et al. 2019). The following hypothesis is therefore proposed:

Hypothesis 3 (H3): Website credibility factors of online consumer reviews have a signifcant impact on consumers purchase intention.

## 3.4.4 Consumer trust and purchase intention

Consumer trust plays a crucial mediating role in the relationship between review credibility, source credibility, website credibility, and consumer purchase intention. When consumers perceive online reviews as credible, they are more likely to develop trust in the product or service, which in turn increases their likelihood of making a purchase (Filieri et al. 2018a, b). Similarly, the credibility of the information source, such as an expert reviewer or an infuencer, enhances consumer trust by reducing uncertainty and perceived risk (Cheung et al. 2009). Furthermore, website credibility— determined by factors such as design, security, and transparency—reinforces trust, thereby strengthening the consumer’s purchase intention (Yaylı and Bayram 2012). Trust acts as a psychological mechanism that converts perceived credibility into purchase behavior by enhancing confdence in the information received (Gefen et al. 2003). Thus, consumer trust serves as a key mediator in the link between review credibility, source credibility, website credibility, and purchase intention.

Hypothesis 4 (H4): Consumer trust mediates the relationship between (review, source, website) credibility and consumer purchase intention.

The research instrument was structured into three sections to ensure a comprehensive understanding of consumer behavior toward online reviews.

(1) Demographic information: The frst section collected demographic details of respondents, including gender, age, educational qualifcation, annual income, and profession.

(2) Consumer preferences: The second section included statements to assess respondents’ preferred e-commerce platforms (Amazon, Flipkart, TATA Cliq, or Paytm Mall), the electronic products for which they frequently search online reviews, and the number of reviews they read before making a purchase decision.

(3) Factors influencing purchase intentions: The third section examined various factors afecting consumer purchase intentions toward online consumer reviews. It included three key dimensions:

Online Review Credibility-related Factors (15 statements)

Reviewer Credibility-related Factors (5 statements) E-commerce Website Credibility-related Factors (7 statements)

A 5-point Likert-type scale was employed in the survey, where 1 = ”Strongly Agree” and 5 = ”Strongly Disagree.” The decision to use a 5-point Likert scale was based on the fndings of the pilot study, which indicated that a 5-point scale provided better response clarity, reduced respondent fatigue, and improved reliability compared to scales with more options. The pilot study results showed that respondents found it easier to distinguish between response categories, leading to more consistent and meaningful data. The questionnaire incorporated both multiple-choice and open ended questions.

The inclusion of open-ended questions in the questionnaire was intended to capture qualitative insights that could complement the quantitative fndings. To ensure a systematic approach to analyzing these responses, we employed qualitative content analysis, wherein responses were carefully reviewed, coded, and categorized into key themes. This method allowed us to identify patterns, underlying motivations, and consumer perceptions that may not have been fully captured through structured multiple-choice questions. By integrating these qualitative insights with the quantitative data, we were able to provide a more comprehensive understanding of consumer behavior. Additionally, this approach helped validate and contextualize the statistical fndings, ofering a richer and more nuanced perspective on the factors infuencing purchase intentions toward online consumer reviews.

## 5.1 Data collection and analysis method

India’s e-commerce industry has experienced substantial growth, with a notable 80% increase in online sales in 2020 (Times of India, 2022). While metropolitan (tier-1) cities have long dominated online shopping trends, recent research highlights a signifcant shift in market dynamics favoring tier-2 cities:

## 5.2 Growing e‑commerce market in Tier‑2 Cities

The market share of tier-2 cities has expanded, while tier-1 cities saw a decline of 9% in 2022 (Unicommerce 2022). Young consumers in tier-2 cities spend an average of 2 h and 25 min per week shopping online, allocating 16% of their income to e-commerce purchases (CyberMedia Research 2023). These cities represent an emerging commercial market, attracting the attention of marketers due to their rapid e-commerce adoption (Kaur & Thakur 2021).

## 5.3 Consumer behavior diferences from Tier‑1 cities

Tier-2 consumers exhibit distinct shopping attitudes, heavily relying on online customer reviews to make purchase decisions (Statista 2023). Unlike metropolitan consumers who have long-established digital shopping habits, tier-2 consumers are still adapting, making it essential to understand their unique preferences and trust factors in online retail.

## 5.4 Diverse and expanding consumer base

According to Indian online real estate platform 99acres.com in 2023, with 97 tier-2 cities identifed, major hubs such as Amritsar, Bhopal, Bhubaneswar, Chandigarh, Faridabad, Ghaziabad, Jamshedpur, Jaipur, Kochi, Lucknow, Nagpur, Patna, Raipur, Surat, Visakhapatnam, Agra, Ajmer, Kanpur, Mysuru and Srinagar are witnessing remarkable e-commerce growth. These cities represent a mix of traditional and digital-frst consumers, making them crucial for businesses looking to expand beyond metro markets (Purohit 2023). While the study provides valuable insights into consumer behavior in tier-2 cities, we acknowledge certain limitations in generalizability that are discussed in limitation section.

The study utilized a non-probability sampling method, most likely purposive or convenience sampling, as respondents were selected based on their relevance to the research— specifcally, consumers in tier-2 cities who engage with online product reviews. Given practical constraints such as response rates and participant availability, the sample size of 227 provides a robust representation of the target population. ## 7 Findings and discussion

## 7.1 Website experience

The website experience of consumers is dependent on reviews posted online on various e-commerce websites. Past research has shown that high-quality reviews written on websites strongly infuence the purchase intention of consumers (Lee & Shin 2014). Not only the quality of reviews but also the e-commerce platform has a signifcant infuence on consumers’ decision-making (Xiao et al. 2022; Sudirjo et al. 2023). A seamless website experience that integrates credible reviews, user-friendly navigation, and secure transaction processes can enhance consumer trust, leading to higher purchase intention (Akdim 2021).

According to the results of the present study, the most important factor in determining the infuence of OCRs on consumer purchase intention for electronic products is the website experience dimension. Variables loading on this factor measure how websites are a useful and powerful platform to infuence the purchase intention of consumers for buying electronic products. The result depicts that website has become a strong preference for consumers to obtain valuable information for electronic product features, quality, price, etc. especially when they have no or less knowledge about them. Website experience consists of 13 variables showing the highest percentage of variation explained in Table 5. The percentage of variance explained by the frst factor is 36.310%. This factor includes various subfactors like website accessibility (signifcance 0.908), information search (signifcance 0.905), website trustworthiness (signifcance 0.884), website reliability (signifcance 0.865), source expertise (significance 0.839), reduce risk (significance 0.795). detailed review (signifcance 0.781), source communication style (signifcance 0.766), product evaluation (signifcance 0.760), website perception (significance 0.759), review consistency (signifcance 0.679), review ratings on websites (signifcance 0. 566), and comparison between website (signifcance 0.521).

From a managerial perspective, e-commerce businesses should focus on optimizing key website experience factors, such as accessibility, trustworthiness, and review credibility, to maximize consumer engagement. Ensuring that reviews are presented transparently, incorporating expert opinions, and ofering comparative product evaluations can further enhance consumer confdence.

## 7.2 Review reliability

Consumers fnd it difcult to make purchase decisions in the presence of fake online reviews generated on websites (Malbon 2013). Fake reviews create uncertainty, making it difcult for consumers to diferentiate between genuine user experiences and deceptive promotional content (Munzel 2016). Consequently, various attributes of Online Consumer Reviews (OCRs), such as reviewer characteristics (age, occupation, gender, expertise), review valence (positive, negative, or neutral), and website reputation, play a crucial role in determining the credibility and reliability of reviews (Filieri et al. 2018a, b). Research suggests that verifed reviewers and detailed profles enhance consumer trust, as they provide transparency regarding the source of the review (Mudambi & Schuf 2010). Additionally, website reputation—established through factors like long-term reliability, security, and transparency—further infuences consumer reliance on OCRs (Baek et al. 2012). The next signifcant factor in assessing the infuence of OCRs on consumer purchase intention for electronic products is review reliability. The percentage variance explained by the second factor is 9.779%. The second factor includes subfactors like reviews from anonymous (signifcance 0.819), source homophily (signifcance 0.648), both positive and negative reviews i.e., review sidedness (signifcance 0.586), and website reputation (signifcance 0.568).

From a managerial perspective, e-commerce platforms should prioritize measures that enhance OCR credibility. Implementing verified reviewer badges, AI-driven fake review detection systems, and transparency in disclosing reviewer details can signifcantly improve consumer trust.

## 7.3 Risk reduction

Consumers rely on Online Consumer Reviews (OCRs) as a crucial tool to mitigate risk and uncertainty before making purchase decisions, particularly for high-involvement products like electronics (Yang et al. 2016). Given the complexity and potential fnancial risk associated with electronic products, consumers seek recent, relevant, and balanced information to make informed choices. The present study identifes risk reduction as the third most important factor infuencing consumer purchase intention, with a percentage variance explained of 7.240%. This fnding aligns with previous research indicating that perceived risk negatively afects purchase decisions in online shopping, and consumers actively seek information that minimizes uncertainty (Chang & Wu 2012).

The risk reduction factor is primarily driven by review recency (signifcance 0.740) and negative reviews discourage (signifcance -0.629). Recent reviews are considered more relevant and credible, as they refect up-to-date product performance and reliability, reducing the uncertainty surrounding electronic purchases (Filieri 2016). On the other hand, negative reviews serve as a crucial warning signal, discouraging potential buyers by highlighting product faws or poor user experiences (Cheung et al. 2009). However, prior research suggests that a mix of positive and negative reviews enhances overall credibility, as overly positive reviews may be perceived as manipulated or fake (Luca & Zervas 2016).

From a managerial perspective, e-commerce platforms should prioritize review transparency and accessibility by prominently displaying recent reviews and ensuring a balance of both positive and negative feedback. Implementing AI-driven fltering systems to highlight the most relevant, authentic, and helpful reviews can further aid consumers in risk reduction.

## 7.4 Evaluation of product or service

In the digital era, Online Consumer Reviews (OCRs) play a vital role in shaping consumer evaluations and purchase decisions by ofering insights in multiple formats, including text, images, and videos (Srivastava & Kalro 2018). The availability and accessibility of these diverse review formats help consumers assess product quality, functionality, and reliability before making a purchase. The present study identifes product evaluation as the fourth most signifcant factor infuencing consumer purchase intention, explaining 6.739% of the total variance in OCRs. This fnding aligns with previous research, which emphasizes that review quantity, format, and quality collectively enhance consumers’ ability to make informed decisions (Mudambi & Schuf 2010).

The product evaluation factor consists of three key subfactors: review quantity (signifcance 0.704), review format (signifcance 0.673), and review quality (signifcance 0.563). Prior research suggests that a greater number of reviews increases perceived credibility, as a high volume of user feedback reduces uncertainty and provides diverse perspectives on product performance (Chevalier & Mayzlin 2006; Dellarocas et al. 2007). Additionally, review format plays a crucial role in information processing, as consumers engage diferently with text-based reviews, visual testimonials, and video demonstrations (Park & Nicolau 2015). Research indicates that consumers perceive video reviews as more authentic and informative due to their ability to showcase real-world product usage, leading to higher trust and stronger purchase intentions (Cheng et al. 2022). Finally, review quality—determined by the depth, clarity, and informativeness of user-generated content—directly infuences consumer trust and decision-making (Filieri 2016). Wellstructured and detailed reviews provide diagnostic value, enabling consumers to make accurate product evaluations and reducing perceived risk (Baek et al. 2012).

From a managerial perspective, e-commerce platforms and businesses should focus on enhancing OCR engagement by encouraging customers to leave detailed and multimedia-rich reviews. Implementing incentive programs for high-quality reviews, providing structured review submission formats, and leveraging AI-driven content moderation to highlight the most insightful reviews can signifcantly improve consumer trust and product evaluation experiences.

## 7.5 Motivation to purchase

Consumers frequently encounter positive recommendations while searching for product information through Online Consumer Reviews (OCRs), which significantly influence their motivation to make a purchase. The valence of reviews—whether they are positive, negative, or neutral— has been widely recognized as a key determinant of consumer attitudes and purchase decisions (Purnawirawan et al. 2015; Hong & Pittman 2020). The fndings of this study position motivation to purchase as the ffth most signifcant factor, explaining 4.525% of the total variance of OCRs. Specifcally, positive reviews (signifcance 0.857) play a crucial role in attracting potential customers, enhancing trust, and ultimately motivating them to complete a purchase.

Prior research suggests that positive reviews enhance consumer confdence by reducing perceived uncertainty and risk associated with online purchases (Chevalier & Mayzlin 2006). Consumers tend to associate positive recommendations with higher product quality and better user satisfaction, reinforcing their intention to buy (Lin & Xu 2017). According to the social infuence theory, individuals are more likely to adopt behaviors endorsed by others, particularly when the reviews are perceived as credible and authentic (Sherif 1936; Cialdini & Goldstein 2004). Additionally, research indicates that emotionally charged positive reviews, which highlight personal experiences and product benefts, are more persuasive in shaping consumer perceptions than neutral or factual reviews (Berger & Schwartz 2011).

From a managerial perspective, businesses should leverage the power of positive OCRs by actively encouraging satisfed customers to leave reviews and testimonials. Implementing review request strategies via post-purchase emails, ofering incentives for detailed feedback, and featuring toprated reviews prominently on product pages can signifcantly enhance consumer motivation.

## 7.6 Improved Trust

Trust plays a pivotal role in shaping consumer reliance on Online Consumer Reviews (OCRs), as the identity of the reviewer signifcantly infuences perceptions of credibility and authenticity (Hernández-Ortega 2018). Consumers are more likely to trust reviews that provide identifiable reviewer information, such as a real name or email ID, as opposed to anonymous or pseudonymous reviews (Chakraborty & Bhat 2017). The present study identifes improved trust as the sixth key success factor, explaining 4.133% of the total variance in OCRs. Specifcally, reviews featuring real names and email IDs (significance 0.764) are perceived as more credible and exert a greater infuence on consumer purchase decisions.

Prior research highlights that source identity strengthens perceived credibility by reducing skepticism about review authenticity and mitigating concerns over fake or manipulated reviews (Baek et al. 2012). Studies suggest that when reviewers disclose their personal details, consumers perceive the information as more trustworthy, transparent, and unbiased, thereby enhancing the review’s persuasive power (Forman et al. 2008). Additionally, research on electronic word-of-mouth (eWOM) suggests that consumers are more infuenced by reviews where the source appears knowledgeable, experienced, and relatable, further reinforcing the importance of reviewer identity (Hu et al. 2012).

From a managerial perspective, e-commerce platforms should prioritize verifed review systems that encourage users to provide real names and identifable credentials while ensuring privacy protection. Implementing “verifed purchaser” labels, integrating profle authentication mechanisms, and featuring reviewer reputation scores can further boost review trustworthiness.

## 7.7 Ease of purchase

Online Consumer Reviews (OCRs) on e-commerce platforms serve as a crucial decision-making tool by providing authentic, user-generated feedback, thereby simplifying the purchase process for consumers (Filieri et al. 2018a, b). This study identifes ease of purchase as the seventh most signifcant factor, explaining 7.240% of the total variance in OCRs. The primary subfactor, website efectiveness (signifcance 0.866), plays a critical role in enhancing purchasing convenience by ofering well-organized, easily navigable platforms where consumers can efciently access relevant product reviews, compare options, and make informed decisions.

Prior research supports the notion that website efectiveness, including features such as structured review presentation, search flters, and interactive review sorting, reduces cognitive effort and enhances decision confidence (Park & Lee 2009). Efective OCR systems allow consumers to quickly identify high-quality and relevant reviews, making the purchase process less time-consuming and more seamless (Mudambi & Schuf 2010). Furthermore, review credibility and ease of access have been linked to increased purchase intention, as they reduce uncertainty and foster trust in both the product and the platform (Cheung et al. 2012).

From a managerial standpoint, e-commerce businesses should focus on optimizing website efectiveness by incorporating AI-driven review fltering, improving review visibility, and providing summarized review insights through sentiment analysis. Features like verifed purchase tags, star rating distributions, and review helpfulness indicators can further enhance usability and trust.

To maximize the impact of OCRs, marketers, e-commerce platforms, and businesses should implement actionable strategies that enhance credibility, trust, and user engagement. By focusing on key factors such as review quality, recency, and authenticity, companies can improve the consumer decision-making process and drive higher conversions.

For marketers and sellers of electronic products, it is crucial to encourage and incentivize detailed reviews, as longer and more comprehensive reviews increase perceived credibility. Brands can achieve this by ofering discounts, loyalty points, or early access to new products in exchange for wellstructured, informative reviews. Partnering with infuencers and industry experts can further enhance review quality, as expert opinions carry signifcant weight in shaping consumer trust. Additionally, brands should proactively monitor reviews using AI-driven sentiment analysis, allowing them to respond efectively to customer concerns, reinforce positive feedback, and manage negative reviews. Insights gained from OCRs can also be leveraged for product development, helping brands refne their oferings based on direct consumer feedback.

E-commerce platforms play a critical role in shaping the credibility of OCRs by improving the visibility and accessibility of recent and high-quality reviews. Given that review timeliness infuences purchase decisions, platforms should implement features that highlight recently posted reviews, ensuring that consumers have access to the most up-to-date product insights. Enhancing review sorting and fltering tools—such as verifed purchase labels, sentimentbased sorting, and relevance rankings—can help consumers quickly identify the most useful reviews. AI-powered review summaries that provide concise insights into key pros and cons can also simplify the decision-making process, reducing cognitive overload. Furthermore, e-commerce platforms must strengthen their fraud detection mechanisms by using machine learning algorithms to identify and flter out fake or manipulated reviews, thereby improving trust and reliability.

Businesses and industry practitioners should optimize their website design to ensure that customer reviews are prominently displayed on product pages. Integrating interactive review elements, such as video testimonials, Q&A sections, and expert endorsements, can enhance user engagement and provide richer insights. Leveraging data analytics to track consumer browsing behavior and interactions with reviews can also help businesses ofer personalized product recommendations, improving customer satisfaction. Trustbuilding initiatives, such as verifying reviewer identities and prominently featuring “verifed buyer” tags, can further reinforce the credibility of OCRs and encourage higher consumer confdence in online purchases.

By implementing these strategic recommendations, marketers, e-commerce platforms, and businesses can harness the full potential of OCRs to build consumer trust, enhance purchase decision-making, and drive long-term brand loyalty in the highly competitive e-commerce landscape.

## 10 Limitations and future research directions

Despite providing valuable insights into online consumer reviews (OCRs) related to electronic products on e-commerce websites, this study has several limitations that should be acknowledged.

Firstly, the study encompasses multiple e-commerce platforms, including Amazon and Flipkart, without diferentiating between their unique interfaces, consumer engagement mechanisms, and review moderation policies. Future research could focus on a single e-commerce platform to yield more precise and platform-specifc insights. Additionally, this research covers various categories of electronic products, potentially diluting category-specifc fndings. Future research could focus on individual product categories (e.g., smartphones, laptops, or home appliances) to understand how credibility factors and consumer responses vary within specifc segments. Additionally, comparing electronic products with other categories such as fashion, beauty, or healthcare could reveal industry-specifc review dynamics.

Secondly, the study’s respondent selection was based on engagement with online product reviews, introducing a bias toward digitally active consumers. As a result, it may not fully represent all consumers, particularly those in tier-2 cities who are less engaged with online platforms. Additionally, the reliance on non-probability sampling limits the generalizability of fndings, as the sample may not accurately refect the broader Indian consumer population.

The insights derived may not be fully applicable to tier-1 cities or rural areas, where shopping behaviors and access to e-commerce platforms difer. While the study includes respondents from multiple tier-2 cities, variations in income levels, digital adoption, and cultural preferences could infuence consumer behavior. A broader, more diverse sample would enhance representation across geographic and socioeconomic segments. Future research should consider probability-based sampling methods and comparative studies across tier-1, tier-2, and rural areas, as well as across diferent countries, could help identify region-specifc trends and consumer trust dynamics.

Thirdly, consumer biases and psychological factors afecting the perception of OCRs were not explicitly addressed. For instance, loyal customers of a particular brand may exhibit resistance to negative reviews due to brand trust and personal biases, which may infuence their purchase decisions diferently compared to new or undecided customers. Future research could explore consumer loyalty and its role in shaping purchase intentions in response to both positive and negative reviews.

Fourthly, the present study utilized factor analysis to identify key dimensions infuencing consumer trust in online reviews, ensuring measurement validity and reliability. Given the study’s objectives and dataset, factor analysis was deemed suitable for extracting underlying patterns. However, future research could incorporate Structural Equation Modeling (SEM) to compare results, as SEM provides a more comprehensive framework by simultaneously estimating relationships between variables while accounting for measurement errors. This comparison could enhance the robustness and generalizability of insights into consumer behavior.

Moreover, the study did not account for the impact of visual elements in reviews, such as profle photos. Profle images may reveal the appearance, lifestyle, age, or gender of the reviewer, potentially leading to homophily efects, where consumers are more likely to trust reviewers who appear similar to them. Future studies could examine how these visual cues infuence trust and purchase decisions.

Another limitation pertains to the prevalence of fake and manipulated OCRs. This study did not specifcally assess the credibility of reviews or the extent to which fabricated reviews impact consumer trust. Given that misleading information can create fear and trust issues among consumers, future researchers should evaluate strategies for identifying and mitigating the infuence of fraudulent reviews.

Lastly, the demographic composition of respondents skewed toward younger consumers, predominantly aged between 21 to 30 years. While this age group constitutes a signifcant portion of e-commerce users and is more accustomed to digital platforms, their responses may not be entirely refective of the broader consumer base. Future research should aim for a more balanced age distribution to capture perspectives from older demographics, who may have diferent trust levels, purchasing habits, and review engagement behaviors. Diferences in trust levels, purchasing habits, and review reliance between younger and older consumers could ofer valuable insights into targeted e-commerce strategies.

By addressing these limitations, future studies can enhance the depth and applicability of research on OCRs and consumer behavior in the e-commerce sector.
