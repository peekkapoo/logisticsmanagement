# Modeling a value-attitude-behavior framework and personality traits: examining consumers’ green purchase behavior for environmentally friendly technology products

## Abstract

Considerable research has been conducted on evaluating environmentally friendly products and consumer behavior. However, there is a paucity of substantial research investigating consumers’ environmental consciousness to predict their attitudes toward environmental concerns and green purchasing behavior. Therefore, we investigate the interrelationship between environmental consciousness and green purchasing behavior, examining how environmental concern mediates this relationship and how personality traits moderate it. The researchers used a crosssectional research design. We employed a survey method to collect data from a sample of 351 consumers of energy-eficient electronic products in Pakistan. The PLS-SEM technique was employed for data analysis. The results showed that environmental consciousness has a significant impact on green purchasing behavior. The mediation efects of environmental concerns and the moderation efects of personality traits were also statistically significant. Within the framework of the VAB model, this study represents an early endeavor to clarify the efect of environmental consciousness on green purchasing behavior, taking into account the mediating efect of consumers’ environmental concerns. The implications of the research are discussed. Our study validated the VAB model in the domain of green consumer behavior.

Keywords Environmental consciousness, Green purchase behavior, Environmental concern, Personality traits, Value-attitude-behavior (VAB)

## 1 Introduction

People around the globe, especially in developing countries, have a high dependency on their natural environment for better livelihoods and health. A well-nurtured and protected natural environment provides necessities for life, including air, water, and food (Kim & [36]). Furthermore, it ofers greater opportunities for economic growth and resilience against natural disasters. Consequently, environmental quality and opportunities significantly determine the well-being of developing countries [10]. However, the world, particularly the developing world, faces devastating environmental challenges and overutilization of natural resources [99]. These issues further escalate the environment’s vulnerability and increase poverty, suggesting a link between green purchase behavior (hereafter GPB), which will protect the environment and strengthen economic wellbeing [15, 30].

Under the prevailing circumstances, fostering eco-friendly consumer purchasing habits can safeguard the natural environment and resources in developing nations [111]. Green purchasing behavior refers to the acquisition of goods and services that are environmentally sustainable and do not deplete or harm natural resources [11, 100]. Given the devastating environmental and economic challenges, consumers around the globe, particularly in developed countries, have increasingly begun purchasing green products [27].

Pakistan is one of the developing countries that relies heavily on its natural environment and resources to sustain and grow. Nevertheless, Pakistan is facing severe challenges related to environmental and natural resources [64]. The sharp rise in Pakistan’s population, currently at 217 million, exacerbates the seriousness of the issue and complicates eforts to manage and protect the natural environment. Additionally, Pakistan generates approximately 50,000 metric tons of waste daily, with only 5% of it being recycled. These environmental degradations have placed Pakistan among the top ten countries most afected by climate change [92]. Environmental activist states, “We need a radical shift in one’s mindset; this will install a new filter of vision through which future choices can be made that are safer, wiser, and truly environmentally friendly” [92].

The legal and legislative environment is quite favorable for GPB in Pakistan; for example, Pakistan adopted the Pakistan Climate Change Act in 2017 [62]. Pakistan has established the Pakistan Climate Change Council (PCCC) and the Pakistan Climate Change Authority (PCCA), which provide essential support and infrastructure to promote sustainable behavior in production and consumption [50]. Furthermore, some environmental activists recently observed Pakistan’s climate march on 20 September 2019 [2]. These legislations and activities indicate an opportunity for the country to produce green products and increase the consumption of environmentally friendly products and services. However, these eforts cannot yield results until consumers’ purchasing behavior is improved and changed to prioritize the adoption and purchase of green products. Subsequently, a developing country like Pakistan needs further exploration and understanding of consumer GPB.

The existing literature examines factors that promote the public’s GPB [13, 86]. For instance, dominant customer logic, e.g., customer values, attitudes, and activities, was more beneficial in predicting consumer behavior than organizational strategies, policies, and practices [12, 39]. The present research employs the theoretical operationalization of the value-attitude-behavior (VAB) model to understand the factors influencing consumers’ green purchasing behavior [45, 48, 85]. Past studies have shown that people hold high values for environmental sustainability, but these values do not always translate into their actions to protect the environment. Hence, this VAB model helps us understand the intervening and contingent factors that convert people’s values into behavior [13].

Past studies have researched the predictors of consumer GPB in the context of developed countries, e.g., the USA [80], Europe [1], UK and China [13, 20], Japan [67] or with a confined sample, e.g., university students [97] utilizing the factors such as care for green products, green marketing orientation, environmental concerns [24, 79]. However, a gap exists in addressing the predictors of GPB in the developing Asian context [49]. There have been fewer studies to understand the mechanisms (intervening factors) or conditions (contingent factors) that can lead to a high value for environmental sustainability, which can ultimately afect consumers’ attitudes toward exhibiting positive green behavior [8, 97]. Hence, based on the operationalization of the VAB Model, environmental consciousness as a value is recognized as one of the primary factors in shaping consumer attitudes towards environmental concerns, which may significantly afect GPB [13]. Environmental consciousness remains significant because it reflects consumers’ positive attitudes toward environmental concerns, including environmental care and societal well-being. Thus, they avoid getting involved in activities that can cause environmental degradation. It is also theoretically conjectured that personality traits interact with environmental consciousness in influencing environmental concerns. Furthermore, human personality determines behavioral actions [13]. Innovative individuals capitalize on new opportunities, indicating a greater inclination towards environmentally friendly purchases. In contrast, individuals who are more reserved, dependable, and disorganized may display less interest in environmental problems and green purchases. For this reason, personality traits could play a moderating role in explaining consumer GPB.

This research intends to contribute to the sustainable marketing domain in four ways. Firstly, it explores how a customer’s environmental consciousness indirectly afects GPB through their attitude towards environmental concerns in a developing country like Pakistan. Secondly, our research examines the moderating influence of personality traits on the relationship between environmental consciousness and attitudes toward environmental apprehension. Thirdly, this study extends the theoretical understanding of the VAB model by introducing the role of personality traits in shaping the relationship between values and attitudes. Lastly, this study provides some insights for practitioners on converting customers’ value for environmental consciousness into GPB in a country like Pakistan, which relies on environmental sustainability for its economic well-being.

We have derived the following research objectives based on the gaps highlighted above. First, this study aims to analyze the impact of environmental consciousness on individuals’ attitudes toward environmental concern and their subsequent purchasing behavior of environmentally friendly products. Second, we examine the impact of individuals’ attitudes toward environmental concern on their GPB and investigate the mediating role of environmental concern in the relationship between environmental consciousness and GPB. Third, the researchers investigate the potential moderating efect of personality traits on the relationship between environmental consciousness and environmental concern.

## 2 Theoretical background and literature review

## 2.1 Value-attitude-behavior (VAB) model

The value-attitude-behavior (VAB) model posits a sequential effect in an individual's decision-making process, originating from values that lead to specific actions [69]. More specifically, it suggests the course of action in the following order: value, attitude, and behavior. In line with this, research indicates that integrating the VAB model with environmental management can lead to consumer behavioral outcomes by elucidating the relationship between values, attitudes, and behaviors [36, 60, 104]. For instance, Verma et al. [106] revealed that values are a vital determinant of consumer attitudes towards green hotels, influencing their intention to visit them.

The categorization of environmental awareness values among consumers has positively influenced their perception of environmental concerns and societal benefits, resulting in a robust positive impact on the adoption of eco-friendly purchasing practices [13]. Therefore, based on the operationalization of the VAB model, this study aims to explore the relationship between environmental consciousness and GPB through consumers’ attitudes toward environmental concerns. Extending the application of VAB in the green consumer behavior perspective: we postulate that internal orientation (i.e., environmental consciousness) among consumers will stimulate the development of an elaborated sense of environmental attitude (i.e., environmental concern) for allowing consumers to behave in eco-friendly ways while purchasing the products and services that are less harmful to the environment (i.e., green product purchase behavior).

## 2.2 Environmental consciousness and green purchase behavior

Environmental consciousness is defined as being aware of the environment and willing to engage oneself to work for its protection. An individual’s psychological response explains his/her propensity to perform environmentally responsible actions [17]. According to Vinson et al. [107], individual values influence the evaluation of product qualities and usage, whereas consumers’ attention to the ecological environment reflects their environmental values [5]. Despite the growing issues of environmental degradation, it is observed that unsustainable buying and consumption patterns are continuously rising at an alarming rate [81]. Therefore, global marketers are raising consumers’ awareness of environmental issues by educating them about their environmental responsibility, increasing their environmental concerns, and highlighting that their unsustainable buving patterns harm the environment. Hence, thev should purchase eco-friendly products that are less environmentally harmful [40]. Furthermore, drawing on the VAB model, the internally oriented values of consumers, such as environmental consciousness, influence their attitudes and motivate them to contribute to environmental sustainability through the purchase of green products. Environmental consciousness leads to internal motivation among consumers to be more concerned about the environment, and consequently, they are more likely to purchase green products.

Moreover, the existing literature has demonstrated that awareness of environmental issues directly impacts consumer behavior [4, 19, 55]. It has been reported that consumers who exhibit a heightened level of environmental consciousness and awareness tend to display a proclivity towards green consumption and show an increased inclination to participate in activities that promote environmental sustainability [51, 54]. The relationship between environmental consciousness and consumer intention to purchase ecofriendly electronic products in Pakistan lacks empirical evidence. Therefore, drawing from the literature and theoretical foundations of the VAB model, an empirical investigation into the efect of environmental consciousness on green purchasing behavior is necessary. Thus, it is hypothesized that:

H1 Environmental consciousness positively influences green product purchase behavior.

## 2.3 Environmental consciousness and environmental concern

Environmental consciousness is a value that reflects a person’s awareness and commitment to reducing environmental harm, emphasizing concerns for maintaining a sustainable environment [16]. It promotes awareness, sympathy, and  concern for the natural environment and issues related to environmental degradation [58]. It involves “thinking ecologically,” a concept of the interdependence between humans and the natural environment [72].

When one has a robust environmental consciousness, they are more likely to demonstrate extra concern and commitment to preserving the natural environment [86]. Moreover, environmentally conscious individuals are more committed to engaging in eco-friendly behaviors, such as the eficient use of natural resources. In line with this assertion, academic literature also documents that environmentally conscious consumers intend to purchase green products [94]. Consumers prioritizing environmental sustainability are more likely to opt for environmentally friendly products that minimize environmental harm. Instead, they prioritize procuring and consuming eco-friendly products, particularly in the Pakistani consumer market [91]. The environmental consciousness of consumers stimulates their desire for a healthy and eco-friendly lifestyle and enhances their inclination to purchase and consume products with organic ingredients [93].

Consistent with VAB theory, consumers' values likely influence their internal orientation. For instance, being aware of environmental issues would motivate consumers to get involved and feel concerned about environmental issues harming the atmosphere and causing pollution. Therefore, environmental consciousness refers to individuals’ concerns about the natural environment [7]. Prior studies remained inconclusive in identifying the efects of environmental consciousness on consumers’ environmental concerns about environmental degradation.

Furthermore, the theoretical lens of the VAB model suggests that environmental consciousness (values) can lead consumers to develop environmental concerns (attitudes) by fostering an elaborated sense of self-actualization among consumers of eco-friendly products. However, there is a need to empirically test this phenomenon, i.e., to what extent Pakistani consumers are conscious and concerned about purchasing and consuming eco-friendly products to preserve the natural environment [86]. Thus, given the assertions and theoretical underpinnings of the VAB model, we believe that environmentally conscious consumers will depict more severe environmental concerns. Hence, we propose the following hypothesis:

H2 Environmental consciousness has a positive influence on environmental concerns.

## 3 Environmental concerns and green purchase behavior

Environmental management literature has regarded environmental concern as a precursor to pro-environmental behavior [53, 59, 89]. Consumers who demonstrate environmental concerns are likely to emphasize the economic and social consequences of environmental degradation [6, 36]. Prior studies suggested that environmental concerns positively impact environmental attitudes [101]. In line with that, recent marketing literature has regarded environmental concerns as a predictor of consumers’ eco-friendly behavior [77].

Similarly, researchers have agreed that environmentally concerned consumers prefer to purchase green products [109]. However, Landry et al. [59] claimed that environmental concerns do not always lead to pro-environmental behavior, whereas Sadiq [88] confirmed the gap between environmental concerns and green product consumption behavior in Pakistan. Young et al. [110] hypothesized that advanced technological equipment in the United Kingdom documented that consumers prefer to adopt green behavior due to their concerns about environmental degradation. Marketing literature further depicts that consumers’ environmental attitudes and concerns shape their buying behavior [61, 105]. Similarly, their purchasing patterns may influence their attitudes and concerns about the natural environment. However, a research gap exists, and existing studies do not adequately address whether consumers’ environmental concerns afect their willingness to purchase energy-eficient electronic products from a theoretical perspective, as outlined in the VAB model [13].

The VAB model suggests that consumers’ internal orientation towards intrinsic values would drive them to act pro-environmentally to prevent environmental degradation. Hence, we propose that consumers’ environmental concerns likely induce GPB. Empirically testing this relationship in the context of Pakistani research may clarify whether environmentally concerned consumers are more likely to purchase energy-eficient electronic products. Thus, based on these assertions and cited literature, we hypothesize that:

## H3 Environmental concern has a positive influence on green purchase behavior.

## 3.1 The mediating role of environmental concern

Environmental consciousness refers to an individual’s awareness, norms, values, and beliefs regarding the minimization of environmental degradation. Environmental concern has been recognized as a crucial relational mechanism that fosters environmentally friendly behavior, positively influencing environmental purchase intentions and explaining pro-environmental behavior [16, 68, 90]. The literature suggests that environmental considerations can notably impact consumers’ inclination to purchase eco-friendly products, as evidenced by studies conducted by Mohd Suki [73] and Mostafa [76]. Similarly, scholarly literature has established a correlation between environmental concern and pro-environmental attitudes and behavioral responses [56, 71].

Moreover, it is reported that environmentally concerned individuals are highly motivated to purchase eco-friendly products and contribute to environmental sustainability specifically in the Pakistani consumer market [74]). The same is evident from the VAB model literature that if an individual becomes environmentally conscious (I.e., Values the Environment), they are inclined to exhibit a pro-environmental attitude and engage in the purchase of eco-friendly products as a means of mitigating the deleterious effects of consumerism on the natural environment [86]. Moreover, consumers’ environmental concerns are indicative of their consumption behaviors. Therefore, it is a crucial determinant in the consumers’ inclination towards buying environmentally friendly products [37]. Therefore, consistent with the VAB model literature, it is expected that environmental consciousness corresponds to increased concern for the environment and, hence, to prevent environmental degradation through engaging in green consumption behaviors, i.e., purchasing green products. Thus, in line with the cited literature and theoretical underpinning of the VAB model, we propose the following hypothesis:

H4 The environmental concern mediates the relationship between environmental consciousness and green purchasing behavior.

## 3.2 Personality traits as a moderator

Recent scholarly literature has indicated that honesty-humility and openness are significant predictors of environmental attitude and behavior [98]. Linking the VAB model with the big five personality traits, the relationship between personality traits and environmental concerns can be justified. Personality traits are transformations in one’s activities under certain related conditions and have been used to determine behavioral responses [46, 75, 95]. “The Big Five personality traits comprise five discrete dimensions: extraversion, agreeableness, conscientiousness, openness to experience, and neuroticism” [18]. Research suggests that agreeableness, conscientiousness, and extraversion are linked to environmental attitudes and behaviors. Therefore, it is considered a significant construct in the study of consumers’ pro-environmental behavior [21, 23, 96, 103].

This interrelationship between personality traits and pro-environmental attitudes and behavior suggests that the expected link between environmental consciousness and environmental concerns can be moderated through personality traits [52]. The theoretical assertions of the VAB model suggest that personality has served as a value to enhance the interaction between environmental consciousness and attitude toward environmental concern, which is expected to act as a driving force to perform GPB [52].

Academic research has provided empirical evidence indicating that attitudes determine pro-environmental behavior. Markowitz et al. [66] conducted a study to examine the impact of openness on pro-environmental behaviors. The research focused on the determinants influencing environmental attitudes and afinity for individuals’ natural environments. Brick and Lewis [9] concluded that the influence of personality traits, such as openness, conscientiousness, extraversion, and self-reported eforts, reduces greenhouse gas emissions.

This effect was observed through the mediating influence of attitudes towards nature. Ojedokun’s [78] study on littering in Nigeria found that personality traits, including altruism and locus of control, influenced self-reported littering behavior. Taken together, theoretical assertions of the VAB model and cited studies provide empirical evidence that personality traits are related to pro-environmental behavior. Furthermore, understanding the interplay between attitude and personality helps promote pro-environmental behavior and environmental sustainability. Developing an attitude towards green concerns may be a gateway to promoting GPB.

According to Wood and Wortman [108] and Prentice et al. [84], personality characteristics afect environmentally responsible and sustainable behavior. A study by Milfont and Sibley [70] established that conscientiousness and agreeableness influence self-described resource-saving responses and environmental assurance. Similarly, Hirsh [44] demonstrated that environmental concern is related to openness, stability, conscientiousness, and agreeableness. Therefore, personality traits are crucial in determining how customers respond to the damaging influence of consumerism on the environment, specifically in the Pakistani context. Therefore, we advocate that personality traits strengthen the association between environmental consciousness and concern. Hence, to understand the underlying mechanism of personality traits in influencing environmental concerns, we propose:

H5 Personality traits moderate the relationship between environmental consciousness and concern.

## 4 Research design

The study utilized a cross-sectional research design to examine how environmental concern mediates this relationship and how personality traits moderate it.

This study has considered urban consumers as the target population who purchase environmentally friendly products, such as hybrid cars, green smart electronic devices, and eco-friendly electronic products for environmental protection and resource conservation. Furthermore, they have also selected hybrid and semi-hybrid cars, most of which are imported and associated with brands such as Toyota, Honda, and Suzuki. Furthermore, the study employed purposive sampling due to the nature of the research, its objectives, and the study design. The respondents were selected based on their judgment, which aligned with the study’s objectives. In line with the purposive sampling technique, respondents were approached in shopping malls based on their observable environmentally friendly behavior, such as carrying reusable shopping bags or showing interest in green product sections. During the initial conversation, participants were briefly asked whether they regularly purchase eco-friendly products, support environmental causes, or engage in sustainable practices (e.g., recycling or energy conservation). Only those who confirmed such behaviors were included in the sample, ensuring relevance to the study’s objective of exploring green consumer behavior. The experts appraised the questionnaire and confirmed its appropriateness for the research and respondents. In this regard, a suitable sample size was selected, and questionnaires were distributed to 400 respondents. Considering the rule of thumb by Hair et al. [32], the sample size should be at least 5 times the total number of items or 10 times the total number of items included in this study. Therefore, the total number of items in the current study was 20, constituting 20% of the maximum sample size. However, to make the data more reliable and consistent, we distributed 400 questionnaires and received back 361 questionnaires. However, 10 questionnaires were excluded from the study due to outliers and missing responses. Therefore, we included 351 usable responses in the current analysis. The study concluded with a 90.25% response rate and 87.75% usable and valid responses.

## 4.2 Data collection process

A self-administered survey questionnaire was used to collect the data because it is a quick, eficient, and comprehensive form of data collection. All constructs were evaluated using a five-point Likert scale. Various research studies have highlighted the importance of survey questionnaires for data collection, time eficiency, and cost-efectiveness. The enumerators collected data from consumers located in shopping malls who purchased eco-friendly products. There are several reasons why we only targeted shopping malls for data collection. Firstly, the respondents who visited shopping malls were easy to approach, as they had ample time to respond to the questionnaire while sitting in the food court or relaxing in the passage areas. Secondly, respondents in the mall meet the study’s demographics and are the right audience to respond to energy-eficient products. Thirdly, these respondents are educated enough to compare energy-eficient products and why traditional electronic products are not energy-friendly. Fourthly, people with good income levels in Pakistan visit malls to shop. Therefore, considering the changing trends in Pakistan, where companies launch energy-eficient products, these respondents can correctly answer their environmental concerns and GPB. Fifthly, we selected the respondents from shopping malls to measure each individual’s personality traits and get adequate information.

However, all the participants volunteered to participate in the survey and returned the completed questionnaire onsite. The enumerators thanked the participants for their participation in the research. Ultimately, 351 usable questionnaires were analyzed. To ensure the validity of the items for all constructs, this study tested both convergent and discriminant validity (Hair et al. 2011).

## 5 Analyses and results

The present study analyzed the proposed research framework using partial least squaresstructured equation modeling (PLS-SEM). The goals of this research were to (1) identify potential predictors of GPB and (2) broaden the applicability of the VAB model; therefore, PLS-SEM was selected as suggested by numerous researchers in the fields of marketing and consumer behavior [8, 28, 41]. Consequently, this investigation employed a two-step smart PLS approach for data analysis [31].

## 6 Discussion

Using the theoretical framework of the VAB model, this study aimed to conduct an empirical investigation examining the impact of environmental consciousness on consumer behavior in the context of eco-friendly purchasing. This study is essential to the growing scholarly literature on eco-friendly consumer behavior. Examining the direct relationships between environmental consciousness and the purchase of eco-friendly products was the impetus for this study. This study examined the influence of environmental consciousness on environmental concerns, a topic that has been previously neglected by academic research. This study aimed to examine the impact of environmental concerns on the adoption of ecologically sustainable purchasing practices and identify the most influential factor in consumer attitudes that encourages the purchase of environmentally conscious products. This study examined the moderating efect of personality characteristics on the relationship between environmental consciousness and the adoption of green purchasing behavior.

![](images/5b2a41c8f7f07c9ce10ac4b7d55af33e0acc45b088cc43f327946096a8d26769.jpg)  
Fig. 5 Interaction of personality traits between environmental consciousness and concern

The study’s results indicate a significant statistical correlation between an individual’s level of environmental awareness and their tendency to engage in green purchasing behavior. The findings suggest that an increased environmental consciousness is associated with adopting eco-friendly consumerism. As consumers become increasingly aware of the importance of environmental preservation and protection, they will be more likely to purchase eco-friendly products and adopt a positive attitude toward using energyeficient electronic devices. Our results revealed a statistically significant correlation between environmental awareness and green purchasing behavior. These findings suggest that higher environmental awareness is associated with more environmentally conscious purchasing behavior. When consumers become environmentally conscious about preserving and safeguarding the environment, they become interested in purchasing green products and demonstrate a favorable attitude toward consuming energy-eficient electronics. Considering the positive correlation between environmental consciousness and GPB, consumer goods companies should develop a green image and establish trust in the minds of consumers. As such, customers recognize that the companies are genuinely concerned about the natural environment, and thus, they would behave accordingly [47]. This finding aligns with previous literature that suggested the relationship between customer trust and value as an essential mechanism in value exchanges [3]. Furthermore, previous studies revealed that when consumers become environmentally conscious, they are more likely to engage in eco-friendly behaviors [51, 54].

Similarly, our research demonstrated that environmental awareness substantially explains environmental concerns concerning environmentally friendly products purchased in Pakistan. This finding suggests that environmentally conscious consumers are growing increasingly concerned about environmental issues. The negative environmental impact could be reduced by improving or altering consumers’ consumption patterns and behaviors to safeguard the natural environment. Moreover, customer consciousness is vital for environmental sustainability because environmentally conscious customers display positive responses, such as repurchasing and recommending to others who share their interest and trust in green consumption. These results support findings on environmental consciousness and environmental concerns [86].

Likewise, the current study’s findings highlighted GPB as an outcome of environmental concerns. Our data revealed that when consumers become concerned about environmental issues caused by consumerism, they engage in green consumer behavior and purchase eco-friendly products that are less harmful to the environment. This finding suggests that consumers tend to prefer green products and services when environmental concerns rise. This finding further revealed that environmental concern is a valuable factor that maintains consumers’ commitment to preserving the natural environment through green behaviors. This finding is consistent with Yadav and Pathak [109], who reveal the efects of environmental concern on green behavior.

The present study demonstrates that environmental concern is a crucial mediator between environmental consciousness and green purchasing behavior. This finding suggests that environmentally conscious consumers have become increasingly concerned about environmental degradation issues. Thus, they prefer to buy products and services that are less environmentally harmful. The results provided a clear picture of how consumers’ values shape their attitude towards the environment; consequently, attitude influences their GPB. This study’s findings are consistent with prior research, which suggests that engaging in environmentally conscious actions fosters environmental concerns and promotes pro-environmental behaviors [90].

We assessed the moderating efects of personality traits to better understand how environmental consciousness predicts environmental concern. Our data revealed that personality traits significantly moderate the link between environmental consciousness and environmental concern. This finding suggests that extroverted consumers are more compassionate and concerned about others and are likely to focus on environmental issues. This finding also aligns with previous studies that suggested personality traits predict consumers’ behavior toward the environment [22].

Ultimately, based on the current study’s findings, it is essential to acknowledge that Pakistan is a developing country that heavily relies on its natural environment and resources to sustain itself and foster growth. Nevertheless, Pakistan is facing severe challenges related to environmental and natural resources. The sharp rise in Pakistan’s population, currently at 217 million, exacerbates the seriousness of the issue and complicates eforts to manage and protect the natural environment. Moreover, Pakistan’s rising energy prices have led people to purchase energy-eficient products to meet their daily needs. In addition, the increasing pollution and energy waste issues in Pakistan have raised serious concerns for the government and presented new opportunities for marketers to capture the Pakistani market. However, consumers are still not very conscious of the usage and benefits of energy-eficient products; additionally, this is due to the energy crisis in Pakistan and the resulting heavy load-shedding. In the current scenario, this is the only way to save energy if the government and companies could launch more energy-eficient and friendly products to satisfy consumer needs. An increasing population and rising usage may lead to a drastic energy shortage in the future, creating an alarming situation in Pakistan.

## 7 Implications for theory

This research has several theoretical and practical implications in green consumer behavior and environmental management literature. Our study validates the VAB model in the domain of green consumer behavior. According to the VAB model, a sequential efect exists in an individual’s decision-making process, where abstract thought values lead to particular actions [69]. Consistent with this assertion, the current study contributes to the green consumer behavior literature by linking consumers’ values, attitudes, and behaviors to produce green consumer behavior outcomes, specifically purchasing energy-eficient electronic products. Notably, current research has illuminated that consumers’ environmental consciousness (values) afects their GPB (behavior) through environmental concerns (attitudes) when they possess specific personality traits. In this way, our study extends the VAB model by proposing that consumers’ values, attitudes, and behaviors afect their intention to purchase green products.

Past literature has focused on environmental knowledge [40], peer influence [28], and individual values [110] in predicting consumer green purchasing behavior (GPB). However, no eforts have been made to study the role of environmental consciousness in predicting consumers’ GPB. Our study fills this gap by examining the impact of environmental consciousness on GPB, aiming to increase the purchase of energy-eficient electronic products through the VAB model as a theoretical framework. Previous research has focused on the antecedents and outcomes of environmental concerns, including pro-environmental behavior [102], environmental sustainability, and environmental responsibility (Umrani et al. 2020). However, to the authors’ knowledge, no study has investigated GPB as an outcome of environmental concerns related to environmental consciousness. Hence, our study contributes to the growing literature on green consumer behavior by linking environmental consciousness with GPB, mediating environmental concerns. In a way, our study highlights the importance of enhancing environmental concerns through environmental consciousness to promote GPB.

The moderating efects of personality traits were examined to understand the influence of environmental consciousness on GPB through environmental concerns. In this way, our study highlighted the importance of personality traits and how these afect the environmental concerns of eco-friendly electronics product buyers.

## 8 Implications for policy and practice

Our research draws some critical implications for policymakers and green marketers. Given the importance of environmental protection, customers need to consider how they respond to environmentally friendly products. When conscious of environmental protection, customers are concerned about protecting the environment from degradation. Today, the world faces various environmental issues, including the scarcity of natural resources, global warming, and other forms of environmental degradation. Thus, there is an urgent need to overcome these environmental challenges. The outcomes of this study are crucial for consumer goods companies in positioning and promoting their products in a manner that meets customers’ expectations for environmental protection. The mediating efect of environmental concern has been demonstrated to explain how environmental consciousness influences consumers’ intention to purchase energy-eficient electronic products.

This study highlights the environmental benefits of green products, which are expected to enhance environmental consciousness and increase consumer purchase intent. The product display stands in the retail stores should indicate signs that help customers distinguish green products from regular products. Individual shelf spaces for environmentally friendly products will allow them to be conveniently located and distinguished, making them more efective. It increases customer awareness of the green buying concept and afects consumer consciousness about green products. Furthermore, implementing green labels will provide a competitive advantage to companies targeting environmentally conscious consumers. The packaging of green products should be unique and distinctive enough to motivate consumers to develop a purchase intention toward these products. Hence, disseminating suficient and credible information about green products will enhance environmental consciousness and increase the purchase intention for green products. Firms should engage with reference groups, such as celebrity and expert endorsements, in advertisements to address environmental concerns among potential consumers. Companies’ online platforms, including their websites and social media pages, can be vital in communicating with customers to improve their awareness of environmentally friendly practices products. Lastly, sponsoring social events may enhance environmental consciousness and improve attitudes toward environmental concerns, benefiting GPB.

## 9 Limitations and future research agenda

This study was limited to a few cities in Pakistan, and future research may expand it by considering every geographical location in Pakistan to make it more comprehensive. The study was cross-sectional, and longitudinal studies are recommended to further enhance the understanding of the phenomenon. The model can expand its theoretical contributions by incorporating additional environmental values and attitude variables to predict green behavior. It can also consider psychographics, demographics, and geography as moderating variables to better explain environmental responses.
