## A B S T R A C T

Multi-criteria decision-making (MCDM) methods are very useful tools for daily decision-making in different fields. In addition, determining an acceptable solution with respect to diferent factors is certainly a very demanding and dificult task. In this paper, a new Measurement of Alternatives and Ranking according to COmpromise Solution (MARCOS) method for a sustainable supplier selection in the healthcare industry (in a polyclinic) in Bosnia and Herzegovina is developed. The advantages of the developed method are: the consideration of an anti-ideal and ideal solution at the very beginning of the formation of an initial matrix, closer determination of utility degree in relation to both solutions, the proposal of a new way to determine utility functions and their aggregation, the possibility to consider a large set of criteria and alternatives while maintaining the stability of the method. Supplier selection is very important for organizations in the medical industry. Sustainability in the supplier selection process in the medical industry is a strategically important issue, and poorly implemented in the private medical sector. Therefore, the example explains how to use the MARCOS method to select sustainable suppliers in the private medical sector. A case study of a sustainable supplier selection for the healthcare industry (a polyclinic) includes ranking of eight alternatives with regard to 21 criteria for all aspects of sustainability. The results and verification of the new method are carried out throughout a comprehensive sensitivity analysis. 21 scenarios with changes in the weight values of criteria were established. the measurement scale from 1 to 9 was changed to 1–5, a comparison with six other MCDM methods was performed, and it was verified in dynamic conditions which implied a change of the elements of the initial decision-making matrix. All phases of the sensitivity analysis showed the validity of MARCOS method. The obtained results and all scenarios in sensitivity analysis show that A2 remains the best alternative.

## 1. Introduction

Organizations around the world are trying to reduce their negative influence on environmental by using a sustainable supply chain. in achieving that goal, suppliers have an important role in implementing a sustainable supply chain (Suraraksa & Shin, 2019). In order to achieve sustainable supply chain management, it is necessary to performing evaluation and selection of sustainable suppliers. The concept of sustainable supplier selection is the integration of economic and social thinking together with environmental awareness in traditional supply chain management (Sen, Datta, & Mahapatra, 2018). Selection of sustainable supplier plays a huge role in improving the performance of companies and their supply chain (Alkahtani, Al-Ahmari, Kaid, & Sonboa, 2019). In addition, the selection of sustainable supplier has significant strategic importance and is a critical stage for the entire sustainable supply chain (Durmić, 2019). Solving sustainable supplier selection issues play a key role in functioning organizations. Therefore, sustainable supplier selection is required in all companies. Sustainable supplier selection is a challenge because multiple criteria are used in decision making, and often these criteria are conflict each other (Memari, Dargi, Akbari Jokar, Ahmad, & Abdul Rahim, 2019). Sustainable supplier selection includes multi-criteria decision-making (MCDM), therefore decision-maker must to consider and assessing qualitative and quantitative factors (Wang, Yang, & Cheng, 2019).

Economic, social and environmental criteria are used when selecting a sustainable supplier, and the logical solution is to use the multi-criteri decision-making methods (MCDM) (Zhou & Xu, 2018). Multi-criteria decision-making (MCDM) is a very applicable tool for analyzing com: plex real problems because of its ability to evaluate diferent alter natives using certain criteria MCDM methods are used to break com plex problems into smaller parts (Mardani, Jusoh, & Nor. 2015), so tha after analyses, these parts would be put together providing an overal picture of the problem. The decision-making process according to Stojić, Stević, Antuchevičienė, Pamučar, and Vasiljević (2018) requires the prior definition and fulfillment of certain factors, especially when it comes to solving problems in complex areas. The theory of multi-criteria decision-making according to Zavadskas, Stević, Tanackov, and Prentkovskis (2018) holds a special place in the field of science. These methods use the criteria that describe decision-making alternatives. A decision-maker uses MCDM methods to evaluate possible alternative using defined decision-making criteria and assess how alternative fulfill the set goal of decision-making. Recently. MCDM methods have increasingly been used to solve various problems. and some new methods have been developed. All MCDM methods can be grouped as: outranking methods, Synthesis methods and Interactive methods (Allah Bukhsh, Stipanovic, & Klanker, in press). Interactive methods are used to solve the problems of optimization, and a non-dominant solution is sought. Outranking methods take decision maker’s (DM’s) preference and allow the ranking of alternatives (Figueira, Greco, Roy, & Słowiński, 2013). Synthesis methods provide the ability to make decisions when it is necessary to evaluate multiple and often conflicting situations (Simanaviciene, Liaudanskiene, & Ustinovichius, 2012). Th application of the MCDM methods helps the decision-maker to conside different criteria or aims in order to reach a compromise among al possible conflicting parameters (Kumar, Kumar, & Barman, 2018). Al though there are large numbers of MCDM methods available in the literature, it is practically impossible to identify the best method. However, depending upon the type and complexity of the problem under consideration, researchers and practitioners normally select the method to be applied. In this paper, a new method called Measurement of Alternatives and Ranking according to COmpromise Solution (MARCOS) consisting of seven simple steps has been developed, Thi method is based on the measurement of alternatives and their ranking in relation to a compromise solution. The compromise solution includes determination of utility functions according to the distance from antiideal and ideal solutions and their aggregations. The most important contribution of this paper is to enrich the entire field of decision making through the development of the new method that will help decision-makers (DMs) in solving multifaceted problems. In addition the MARCOS method can be a contribution to the Prospective Multipl Attribute Decision Making (PMADM) introduced by Hashemkhan Zolfani, Maknoon, and Zavadskas (2016) since it has so many similarities regarding the main idea of the mentioned approach. The PMADM model considers future limiters in the evaluation process. Each limiter which can occur in the future and within the time period a topic i being explored can be considered in the process of decision-making about a possible future scenario (Hashemkhani Zolfani et al., 2016). In addition no previous studies have considerably signified the im portance of sustainable supplier selection in healthcare industry especially in the private medical industry. Therefore. it is imperative to use MCDM methods to make a sustainable supplier selection, for ex ample by a private organization in the medical industry. In order to address this critical issue, this paper aims to conceive three funda mental queries: (a) can a sustainable supplier selection process b carried out in the healthcare industry?; (b) are there any diferences in the selection of sustainable suppliers in the healthcare sector as com pared to other industries?; (c) can an approach supported by the newl developed MARCOS method be useful for ranking sustainable sup pliers? By addressing all these questions, the possibility to identify an select the most suitable sustainable supplier in the healthcare industr is presented in this paper, thus contributing to the sustainable environment of this ever growing business concern. This is particularly important because of the increasing pressure that all organizations have with regard to the application of environmental standards and corporate social responsibility. These two components have become prerequisites for organizational operations and sustained growth. Based on all the above, the contribution of this paper is reflected in the following:

## 2. Literature review

A sustainable supply chain management (SSCM) is the integration of economic, social and environmental thinking in traditional supply chain (SC) environment. Sustainability in SC arises from product design and development, material selection, manufacturing, packaging, transportation, warehousing, distribution, consumption, return, and disposal (Sen et al., 2018). In order to develop an SSCM, it is necessary to incorporate sustainability measures in the SC processes that link suppliers and customers. Sustainability in the process of procurement in public healthcare organizations is a strategic issue whose popularity has increased in recent decades. Private healthcare organizations have been engaged in sustainable procurement mainly on a voluntary basis (Chiarini, Opoku, & Vagnoni, 2017). Private healthcare organizations are mainly motivated by economic and financial returns for the procurement (Cherrafi, Elfezazi, Chiarini, Mokhlis, & Benhida, 2016). The selection of suppliers is the most important element of any SCM (Puška, Kozarević, Stević, & Stovrag, 2018; Erceg & Mularifović, 2019; Ghorabaee. Zavadskas, Olfat, & Turskis, 2015). Suppliers should deliver the required amount of good quality materials and raw materials on time. Only with such deliveries, the products that will meet the needs of customers can be manufactured. Supplier selection, which will be a strategic partner, is critical for organizations within the medical industry (Nudurupati, Bhattacharya, Lascelles, & Caton, 2015). The selection of suppliers is often based exclusively on the lowest price. This method ignores the direct and indirect costs associated with the quality, delivery and costs of purchased material (Badi & Ballem. 2018). Since uncertainty is perpetually present in business operations, the selection of suppliers cannot be exclusively based on the price in organizations from the medical industry. Other key criteria that affect the selection process are to be simultaneously considered. In traditional SCM, the most important criteria considered for supplier selection are price, costs, quality and delivery (Vasiljević, Fazlollahtabar, Stević, & Vesković. 2018), while in an SSC, economic, social and environmental criteria are taken into account when selecting the suppliers. The reason is to enable the organizations to achieve long-term business sustainability and maintain market position (Sen et al., 2018). The criteria to be used for the selection of suppliers are dependent on certain in dustries and enterprises (Badi, Abdulshahed, & Shetwan, 2018).

Over the last few decades. business organizations around the world have shown an increasing concern about the impact of their manu facturing and other activities on the environment (Vanalle, Ganga, Godinho Filho. & Lucato. 2017). In the medical industry, organizations have concerns for the environment. This concern is even greater takin into account that the medical industry is diferent from other industries since it has patient care as the overriding goal. It is necessary to provid health-friendly and environmental-friendly equipment, and medicines to take care of patients. Therefore, when selecting a supplier, attention has begun to be paid to environmental criteria. Thus, the environmental criteria are integrated in the selection of suppliers (Govindan, Kaliyan Kannan, & Haq, 2014) in order to make an SC green. This led to a gree supplier selection. The selection of a green supplier is a process that formally introduces environmental components into an SC (Maksimović, Osmanović, & Puška, 2017). However, social factors were not included in the selection of a green supplier. By introducing th social responsibility component in a green SC. an SSC has been estab lished. A sustainable supplier selection includes mainly three elements, namely economic criteria (traditional cost-based criteria), environmental criteria (based on environmental impacts) and social criteria (occupational, health and safety issues, psychological state of em ployees and working conditions). The sustainable supplier selection is based on the idea that businesses need to consider environmental and social aspects regarding suppliers rather than focusing only on the economic value of the supplier. This implies that environmental issue need to be solved in a SSC with the fulfillment of societal standards at all levels of the SC (Seuring, 2013). In the case of a sustainable supplier selection, the sustainability factors that apply to the selection of sup pliers are used, where they are assessed on the basis of these factor (Rabbani, Foroozesh, Mousavi, & Farrokhi-Asl. 2019). Imprecise data ambiguity of human judgment and uncertainty among sustainability factors are unavoidable and pose huge challenge to implement accu rately the sustainable selection of suppliers in an SSC (Orji & Wei 2014). The selection of suppliers within a SC was long been considered a problem of multi-criteria decision-making (MCDM) (Sen et al.. 2018) With the introduction of green, i.e. a sustainable supplier selection, the complexity of this decision-making form increases. It is noticeable that the criteria for selecting suppliers have been changed from quantitative to qualitative criteria. so the methods that can be used in the selectior of suppliers have also been changing (Puška, 2015). The selection of suppliers by applying quantitative criteria can be easily solved using traditional MCDM methods They provide an effective framework for addressing the problem of supplier selection where there are conflicting decision-makers’ goals (Zhou & Xu, 2018). With the shift to the appli cation of qualitative criteria in the selection of suppliers, other methods that enable decision-making in the case of inaccurate and unclear in formation have started to be used These methods are based on the application of the theory of fuzzy sets. The application of the theory of fuzzy sets is more similar to human thinking because there are realworld situations that have not been defined and it is difficult to de termine the boundaries of the set (Puška et al, 2018). This is particu larly present when the selection of suppliers is performed using quali tative criteria as it is with a sustainable supplier selection. ## 3. Methods

This section of the paper presents the methodology for selecting a sustainable supplier consisting of three phases as shown in Fig. 1.

Sustainable supplier selection in healthcare industries has been performed throughout three phases. In the first phase, the formation of an expert team is completed and a set of alternatives is defined, i.e. a set of potential suppliers, which represents the formation of a multi-criteria model. Modeling expert preferences in decision-making problems (Naeini, Mosayebi, & Mohajerani, 2019) is an interesting field of research, which has been the subject of studies by numerous authors in the last decade. The weight coeficients obtained in this phase of the model are used further in the MARCOS model for the evaluation of suppliers. ## 3.1. Measurement of alternatives and ranking according to COmpromise solution (MARCOS)

In this section, the algorithm of MARCOS method is presented. The MARCOS method is based on defining the relationship between alternatives and reference values (ideal and anti-ideal alternatives). On the basis of the defined relationships, the utility functions of alternatives are determined and compromise ranking is made in relation to ideal and anti-ideal solutions. Decision preferences are defined on the basis of utility functions. Utility functions represent the position of an alternative with regard to an ideal and anti-ideal solution. The best alter native is the one that is closest to the ideal and at the same time furthest from the anti-ideal reference point. The MARCOS method is performed through the following steps:

Step 2: Formation of an extended initial matrix. In this step, the extension of the initial matrix is performed by defining the ideal (AI) and anti-ideal (AAI) solution (1).

$$
X = \begin{array}{c} A A I \\ A _ {1} \\ A _ {2} \\ \dots \\ A _ {m} \\ A I \end{array} \left[ \begin{array}{c c c c} C _ {1} & C _ {2} & ... & C _ {n} \\ x _ {a a 1} & x _ {a a 2} & ... & x _ {a a n} \\ x _ {1 1} & x _ {1 2} & \dots & x _ {1 n} \\ x _ {2 1} & x _ {2 2} & ... & x _ {2 n} \\ ... & ... & ... & ... \\ x _ {m 1} & x _ {2 2} & ... & x _ {m n} \\ x _ {a i 1} & x _ {a i 2} & \dots & x _ {a i n} \end{array} \right]\tag{1}
$$

The anti-ideal solution (AAI) is the worst alternative while the ideal solution (AI) is an alternative with the best characteristic. Depending on the nature of the criteria, AAI and AI are defined by applying equations (2) and (3):

$$
A A I = \min _ {i} x _ {i j} \quad i f j \in B \quad a n d \quad \max _ {i} x _ {i j} \quad i f j \in C\tag{2}
$$

$$
A I = \max _ {i} x _ {i j} \quad i f j \in B \quad a n d \quad \min _ {i} x _ {i j} \quad i f j \in C\tag{3}
$$

where B represents a benefit group of criteria, while C represents a group of cost criteria.

Step 3: Normalization of the extended initial matrix (X). The elements of the normalized matrix $N = [ n _ { i j } ] _ { m \times n }$ <sub>n</sub>are obtained by applying equations (4) and (5):

$$
n _ {i j} = \frac {x _ {a i}}{x _ {i j}} \quad i f j \in C\tag{4}
$$

$$
n _ {i j} = \frac {x _ {i j}}{x _ {a i}} \quad i f j \in B\tag{5}
$$

where elements $x _ { i j }$ and $x _ { a i }$ represent the elements of the matrix X.

Step 4: Determination of the weighted matrix $V = [ \nu _ { i j } ] _ { m \times n } .$ The weighted matrix V is obtained by multiplying the normalized matrix N with the weight coeficients of the criterion $w _ { j } ,$ equation (7).

$$
v _ {i j} = n _ {i j} \times w _ {j}\tag{7}
$$

Step 5: Calculation of the utility degree of alternatives Ki. By applying equations (8) and (9), the utility degrees of an alternative in relation to the anti-ideal and ideal solution are calculated.

$$
K _ {i} ^ {-} = \frac {S _ {i}}{S _ {a a i}}\tag{8}
$$

$$
K _ {i} ^ {+} = \frac {S _ {i}}{S _ {a i}}\tag{9}
$$

where S $( i \ = \ 1 , 2 , . . , m )$ represents the sum of the elements of the weighted matrix V, equation (10).

$$
S _ {i} = \sum_ {i = 1} ^ {n} v _ {i j}\tag{10}
$$

Step 6: Determination of the utility function of alternatives f(K ). The utility function is the compromise of the observed alternative in relation to the ideal and anti-ideal solution. The utility function of alternatives is defined by equation (11).

$$
f (K _ {i}) = \frac {K _ {i} ^ {+} + K _ {i} ^ {-}}{1 + \frac {1 - f (K _ {i} ^ {+})}{f (K _ {i} ^ {+})} + \frac {1 - f (K _ {i} ^ {-})}{f (K _ {i} ^ {-})}};\tag{11}
$$

where $f ( K _ { i } ^ { - } )$ represents the utility function in relation to the anti-ideal solution, while $f ( K _ { i } ^ { + } )$ represents the utility function in relation to the ideal solution.

Utility functions in relation to the ideal and anti-ideal solution are determined by applying equations (12) and (13).

$$
f (K _ {i} ^ {-}) = \frac {K _ {i} ^ {+}}{K _ {i} ^ {+} + K _ {i} ^ {-}}\tag{12}
$$

$$
f (K _ {i} ^ {+}) = \frac {K _ {i} ^ {-}}{K _ {i} ^ {+} + K _ {i} ^ {-}}\tag{13}
$$

Step 7: Ranking the alternatives. Ranking of the alternatives is based on the final values of utility functions. It is desirable that an alternative has the highest possible value of the utility function.

## 4. Case study of a healthcare organization

The problem of sustainable supplier selection is a very important issue in the medical industry. Therefore, the application of a model based on the new MCDM MARCOS method in sustainable supplier selection is presented in this study. The study demonstrates an empirical illustration of how the MARCOS method can be used in a model of sustainable supplier selection. The problem of sustainable supplier selection was solved by applying group decision-making. An example of Ghetaldus Polyclinic Brčko was used to address this decision-making problem. Ghetaldus Polyclinic Brčko is located in the northeast of Bosnia and Herzegovina and is the only one providing eye surgical interventions in the Brčko District of B&H. The specificity of this polyclinic is that the materials and equipment used here must meet strict healthcare criteria, since they are medical supplies. Therefore, the selection of supplier is of great importance. The research for this study was carried out in the period from February to March 2019. First, the experts who would evaluate the suppliers were identified among the personnel. This study involved three experts working for the Ghetaldus Clinic:

• The first expert has a decades-long experience in leadership and he is a professional in economics with the title of Doctor of Economic Sciences who has spent his entire life in ophthalmology.

• The second expert is a medical professional, a specialist in ophthalmology, with the title of Doctor of Medical Sciences who specializes in pediatric eye diseases.

The third expert is also a medical professional with decades of ex perience in eye surgery, with the title of Doctor of Medical Sciences.

The aim of Ghetaldus Polyclinic Brčko was to modernize its business through procurement of new equipment. In addition, this clinic planned to start cataract surgery in the eye that required new equipment. First, the suppliers responsible for delivering medical equipment were determined. Second, out of the total number of suppliers, eight suppliers who possessed the appropriate equipment were identified. Table 2 presents potential suppliers. Third, data on these suppliers were collected based on communication with other clinics, polyclinics and experts and their experience with those suppliers. Fourth, the sustainability criteria to evaluate the suppliers were defined. Fifth, the suppliers were evaluated by three experts. The process of sustainable supplier selection in the medical industry is not significantly diferent from other sectors of the economy. It is possible to apply same criteria as in other industries. However, it is necessary to put greater emphasis on the social criteria in the medical industry, as patients are at the heart of every medical organization. In the private medical sector, the focus is on economic criteria and less attention is paid to environmental factors (Cherrafi et al., 2016).

In order to perform a sustainable selection of suppliers based on a comprehensive literature review, the criteria shown in Table 3 have been used. The definition of each sub-criterion is provided and references where these criteria have been used so far are given. Each sustainability criterion is composed of sub-criteria, so that total of 21 subcriteria grouped into three main sustainability criteria are used.

DMs evaluated these criteria for each supplier using a scale of five degrees: 1 = very poor; 3 = poor; 5 = medium; 7 = good; 9 = very good. The total number of suppliers was eight, which were evaluated by three DMs.

Ghetaldus Polyclinic Brčko decided to acquire new equipment and faced the problem of selecting a supplier. In order to meet stringent requirements in terms of equipment used in healthcare institutions, a sustainable supplier selection was applied. ## 6. Result validation

In this section of the paper, the validation of the obtained results has been performed. The stability of the MARCOS method is demonstrated throughout a sensitivity analysis that includes a four-phase procedure. The first part is determining the method sensitivity to the change of the weights of criteria. 21 scenarios with diferent values of criteria have been formed. In the second part of the sensitivity analysis, the stability of the MARCOS model is presented in dynamic conditions that involve changing the elements of the initial decision-making matrix. The third part includes changing the measurement scale, while, in the fourth part, a comparison with other methods is made.

## 6.2. The influence of dynamic matrices on rank reversals

Changing certain parameters of decision-making matrix, such as introducing a new or eliminating the existing alternative, can lead to changes in preferences. In the following part, therefore, several scenarios are formed in which the change of the elements of decision-making matrix is simulated. For each scenario, a change in the number of alternatives is made, after which in newly created conditions the MARCOS model is applied. The scenarios are formed in such a way that in each scenario the worst alternative is eliminated from subsequent consideration. At the same time, for each scenario the remaining alternatives are ranked according to the new initial decision-making matrix. This analysis has two objectives: (i) understanding the robustness of the solution obtained in uncertain conditions; and (ii) the analysis of the performances of the MARCOS model in the conditions of a dynamic initial matrix of decision-making. The initial solution applying the MARCOS model is generated as $\mathrm { A _ { 2 } ~ > ~ \mathrm { A _ { 5 } ~ > ~ \mathrm { A _ { 4 } ~ > ~ \mathrm { A _ { 7 } ~ > ~ \mathrm { A _ { 3 } ~ > ~ \mathrm { A _ { 4 } ~ > ~ \mathrm { A _ { 4 } ~ > ~ \mathrm { A _ { 7 } ~ > ~ \mathrm { A _ { 3 } ~ > ~ \mathrm { A _ { 4 } ~ > ~ \mathrm { A _ { 4 } ~ > ~ \mathrm { A _ { 4 } ~ > ~ \mathrm { A _ { 5 } ~ > ~ \mathrm { A _ { 4 } ~ > ~ \mathrm { A _ { 4 } ~ > ~ \mathrm { 0 } ~ } } } } } } } } } } } } } } }$ ${ \bf A } _ { 1 } > { \bf A } _ { 6 } > { \bf A } _ { 8 } .$ . Alternative $\mathbf { A } _ { 8 }$ is identified as the worst, so in the first scenario alternative A8 is eliminated from the set. Thus, a new decision making matrix is obtained with a total of seven alternatives. A new solution is generated and the following preferences are obtained: $\mathrm { ~ \sf ~ A _ { 2 } ~ > ~ \sf ~ A _ { 5 } ~ > ~ \sf ~ A _ { 4 } ~ > ~ \sf ~ A _ { 7 } ~ > ~ \sf ~ A _ { 3 } ~ > ~ \sf ~ A _ { 1 } ~ > ~ \sf ~ A _ { 6 } ~ } $ . The preferences from the first scenario shows that $\mathsf { A } _ { 2 }$ remains the best alternative, while $\mathsf { A } _ { 6 }$ remains the worst alternative. ## 7. Discussion and advantages of the MARCOS method

At the managerial level, goals are defined and optimal alternatives are selected, so the multi-criteria nature of decisions is emphasized. At the managerial level, decision-makers have the power to accept or reject the solution proposed by the level of engineering. In some cases, the structure of preferences is based on political rather than only technical criteria. In these situations, the system analyst can assist the decision making process by applying comprehensive analysis and outlining important characteristics of non-inferior and/or compromise solutions (Zeleny, 1982).

A compromise solution to a problem with conflicting criteria can help decision-makers make the final decision. The basis of the compromise solution was established by Zeleny (1982). A compromise solution is a feasible solution that is closest to the ideal, and compromise represents an agreement that is established by mutual concessions.

Numerous papers have considered the application of multi-criteria models for addressing supplier selection problems in both healthcare industries and other fields (Oruezabala & Rico, 2012). However, as shown in the previous section of the paper, many of the models applied have diferent characteristics (advantages / limitations). In this section, for the purposes of discussing the results, the specific models that are most commonly used to address supplier selection problems are highlighted with reference to healthcare industries. For example, Amindoust et al. (2012) and Azadnia, Saman, and Wong (2015) used the hierarchical models of fuzzy logic system (FLS) for supplier selection. FLS, as artificial intelligence models, enables the storage of expert knowledge in the database of FLS rules. They also provide the possibility of a nonlinear presentation of the criterion function of alternatives with the ability to change the character of the function depending on the expert preferences stored in the rule database. However, a significant limitation of the FLS is the indirect representation of the weighting coefi cients of the criteria through the rule database of the expert system. The developed FLS for supplier selection is usually not suitable for solving other examples and requires changes to the rule database. However, changes to the rule database require specific knowledge in the field of artificial intelligence, which is one of the limitations to the wider application of FLS to solve real-life problems.

In addition, the application of FLS is limited to solving problems that consider a smaller number of criteria, usually up to seven criteria at one hierarchical level. A larger number of criteria complicate the models significantly, so this is a limiting factor for the wider application of FLS to address supplier selection problems. Unlike models that are based on fuzzy logic systems, the MARCOS model is characterized by flexibility, and is suitable for solving multi-criteria models with more criteria. In addition, a significant feature of the model is a simple algorithm that does not become more complex by increasing the number of criteria / alternatives.

In order to address some of the disadvantages of the AHP model, authors have developed hybrid AHP-TOPSIS models to address supplier selection problems in healthcare industries and in other fields (Chauhan & Singh, 2016; Kannan, Khodaverdi, Olfat, Jafarian, & Diabat, 2013; Luthra et al., 2017; Orji & Wei, 2015), as well as new multi-criteria tools such as the MAIRCA method (Chatterjee, Pamucar, & Zavadskas, 2018). In the hybrid AHP-TOPIS models, the AHP model was used to define the weighting coeficients of criteria, while the TOPSIS method was used to evaluate the alternatives. However, in addition to the aforementioned limitations in the implementation of the AHP model there are some limitations to the TOPSIS model. The TOPSIS method introduces the ranking index including the distances from the ideal point and from the negative-ideal point (Hwang & Yoon, 1981; Petković, Madić, Radovanović, & Gečevska, 2017). These distances in TOPSIS are simply summed up without considering their relative im portance. However, the reference point could be a major concern in decision-making, and to be as close as possible to the ideal is the rationale of human choice (Opricovic & Tzeng, 2004). Being far from a nadir point could be a goal only in a particular situation, and the relative importance remains an open issue. The similar problems (like in TOPSIS method) may appear in MAIRCA method. The TOPSIS method uses an n-dimensional Euclidean distance that could represent some balance between total and individual satisfaction, but uses it in a dif ferent way than MAIRCA.

In order to eliminate the above-mentioned disadvantages of the TOPSIS model, some authors (Banaeian, Mobli, Fahimnia, Nielsen, & Omid, 2018; Qin, Liu, & Pedrycz, 2017), in addition to the TOPSIS model, apply TODIM and COPRAS multi-criteria models. COPRAS and TODIM methods, compared to other multi-criteria tools, have a more complex process of fusing the values of alternatives. It especially refers to the TODIM method, which is therefore much less applied in solving multi-criteria problems (Fan, Zhang, Chen, & Liu, 2013). Despite the more complex process of aggregating the values of alternatives, a significant advantage of the COPRAS method is that it does not require a separate transformation of the values of the cost/benefit criteria in a normalized matrix. The total ranking index of each alternative is cal culated using proportional estimation. However, in some situations, the COPRAS method may show some degree of inconsistency. For example, if the value of the dominant alternative for the cost criterion is the lowest and the highest weight of the criterion $w _ { j }$ corresponds to that criterion, then the sum of these weighted values is found in the denominator of the aggregation function. This can lead to misjudgment of alternatives.

Bearing in mind the mentioned problems in certain multi-criteria models used to solve supplier selection problems, there is a need to develop new tools that enable rational and reliable decision-making. In order to improve the robustness of MCDM, a new method, called MARCOS has been developed in this paper, which uses the ratio method and the reference point method to obtain the scheme of basic comprehensive decision information. The MARCOS method is a powerful and robust tool for optimizing multiple goals. Compared with other methods, this method is simple, efective, and easy to sort and optimize the process. MARCOS refreshes the MCDM domain by introducing an algorithm for analyzing the relationship between alternatives and reference points. The MARCOS method integrates three starting points to provide a robust decision: (i) defining reference points (ideal and anti ideal values); (ii) determining the relationship between alternatives and ideal/anti-ideal values; (iii) defining the utility degree of alternatives in relation to the ideal and anti-ideal solution. Also, the results obtained by the MARCOS method are more reasonable due to the fusion of the results of the ratio approach and reference point sorting approach.

MARCOS method shows greater stability compared to TOPSIS method, which is based on similar principles, i.e. defining the distance of alternatives relative to the reference points (ideal and anti-ideal al ternative). In relation to TOPSIS method, MARCOS shows the robust ness of the results when changes are made in the measurement scales of the decision attributes. Moreover, MARCOS method demonstrates the significant reliability of the results in a dynamic environment compared to TOPSIS model. In TOPSIS model, elimination of the worst alternative from a decision-making matrix leads to a change in the existing rank, i.e. the rank reversal problem occurs. On the other hand. in MARCOs method, no such phenomena is observed. Thus, the MARCOS method has shown significant stability and reliability of results in a dynamic environment. Furthermore, it is important to note that in numerous simulations, the MARCOS method has shown stability in processing large data sets. This is validated using the case study discussed in this paper. In further analyses of the results, MARCOS method is compared with other well established MCDM methods. The results shows slight deviation of ranks compared to TOPSIS method, while in comparison with MABAC, SAW, ARAS, WASPAS and EDAS method, MARCOS method shows an extremely high rank correlation. It can be concluded that developed MARCOS method provides information about preferences that contributes to a realistic and stable assessment of decisionmaking problems. This further improves the accuracy and reliability of decision-making results. Considering that the field of MCDM aims to apply reliable and rational models for eficient decision-making, MARCOS method itself imposes a logical choice for future solution of multi-criteria problems.

## 8. Conclusion

The issue of sustainability in the supply chain has become a significant issue that is receiving considerable attention. In the medical industry, greater attention is paid to sustainability by public medical organizations, while, in private medical organizations, revenue is the focus. Therefore, in this paper, it is demonstrated how a private medical organization makes a sustainable supplier selection using MCDM methods. This paper has developed a new method to address the problem of multi-criteria decision-making, called MARCOS. The new developed MARCOS method is based on defining the relationship between alternatives and reference values. Based on such defined relationships, the utility function of alternatives is determined and the ranking in relation to the reference values is performed. In addition, a new model for addressing the problem of sustainable supplier selection is developed, based on MARCOS method. In total, eight suppliers were considered. Each of the sustainability criteria was subdivided into seven sub-criteria. Based on the experts’ evaluation, in this study, the priority was given to the economic sustainability criteria, then social, while the least importance was given to environmental sustainability criteria. Of all the sub-criteria used in this study, the highest significance was given to the criterion of quality, then price, which were separated from other criteria. This is due to the fact that the best quality at the best price is always required. This does not mean that other criteria are irrelevant. It is also necessary to use other criteria to select a supplier who best helps in doing business in Ghetaldus Polyclinic Brčko. The applied model has exploited the advantages of the MARCOS method to make a sustainable supplier selection based on the medical industry example. The MARCOS method, its simplicity, stability, and precision has been used. Thus, the benefits of the model are best utilized and demonstrated in the medical industry, while, at the same time, without modifications in other industries.

In the proposed model based on the example from the practice, it is shown how MCDM methods can be used in the sustainable selection of suppliers in the medical industry. The developed method has shown a great degree of flexibility and it can be used in other branches of economy and for other real problems. It should then be adapted to the needs of managers from those branches and the issues considered. Thus, this model can be applied to improve the competitiveness of those companies. The developed model is based on expert knowledge and on the basis of expert assessments it can rank certain alternatives. However, it is possible to use real data for the assessment of alternatives since the MARCOS method is not limited to certain scales. It can help the DMs to use this method in any situation when it is necessary to rank certain alternatives.

The main points of this paper are as follows:

\- The development of a new method of multi-criteria analysis based on hierarchical and methodological procedures for solving realworld business problems, and in this study applied to a sustainable supplier selection in the medical industry.

\- MARCOS method is based on testing the reference values of alternatives in relation to ideal values and on a comprehensive rational and reasonable application methodology.

\- The application of a decision-making model based on MARCOS method was tested by qualitative analysis and it helped to classify suppliers from the best to the worst by applying sustainability criteria.

\- The model is very flexible and simple so it can also be applied to other problems of multi-criteria analysis (in medical and other sectors of the economy).

\- MARCOS method provides the ranking of alternatives, and helps managers and all other decision-makers to address the problem easily and solve it by applying the ranking of alternatives.

\- The applied MCDM-based model has shown good performance in a sustainable supplier selection.

MARCOS method was tested using various instruments. First, it was compared with the TOPSIS method by applying the testing of the impact of dynamic matrices on the rank reversal. The results showed that with the TOPSIS method there was a change of ranking order using the $S _ { 3 ^ { - S _ { 7 } } }$ scenarios, while that was not the case with the MARCOS method. Thus, the robustness and stability of the MARCOS method in a dynamic environment was confirmed. Second, by changing the measurement scale, the MARCOS method has shown that it is not sensitive to the change of diferent measurement scales. It has been shown that certain changes in the value of the elements occur, but the ranks have remained the same. This has confirmed the stability and precision of the method. Third, compared to other previously developed methods, MARCOS method has provided an identical ranking order of alternatives. The only method that deviates from all others is TOPSIS method. Through diferent sensitivity analyses and performance comparisons, it has been demonstrated that the MARCOS method shows good stability in the assessment of the decision-making problem. MARCOS method can easily be implemented to address a wider range of multi-criteria problems in other industries like logistics and transportation (for vehicle selec tion), life cycle management, materials selection, location selection problems, manufacturing process evaluation, technology evaluation to name a few. This method can also be expanded through fuzzy or rough extensions for coping uncertainties and to use linguistic values in order to consider to human judgments more comprehensively. It is also possible to use this method in conjunction with other subjective and ob jective criteria weighting tools.

It is also expected that the outcomes of this paper would suit to the long term plans and goals of healthcare organizations in diferent ways. It is expected that the proposed decision-making model would improve the dynamics and economic movement inside the medical industries and healthcare organizations. It would also inspire the organizational networks to enhance business competitiveness, economic activity and employment. Moreover, proper balancing of the three pillars i.e. economic, environmental, and social criteria would increase the sustain able activities in the medical industry.The proposed model can be implemented by the policy makers as a systematic foundation in decision analysis for efective formulation of healthcare and medical strategies.
