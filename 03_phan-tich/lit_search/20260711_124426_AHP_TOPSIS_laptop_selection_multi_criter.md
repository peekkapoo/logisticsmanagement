> By breaking down the decision-making problem into a hierarchy of more easily comprehended sub-problems, AHP enables decision-makers to systematically evaluate the relative importance of various criteria and actions. While the Analytical Hierarchy Process (AHP) provides a structured approach for multi-criteria decision-making (MCDM), TOPSIS [20, 21] is another robust MCDM technique that ranks alternatives using multiple criteria based on their relative closeness to an ideal solution.

Unlike AHP, which involves pairwise comparisons and eigenvector calculations, TOPSIS evaluates alternatives based on their geometric distance to a positive ideal solution (PIS) and a negative ideal solution (NIS).

> In this study, we select a combination of fuzzy versions of MCDM methodologies (AHP and TOPSIS) due to their complementary strengths.
