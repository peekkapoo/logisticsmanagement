\section{TOPSIS Step-by-Step Calculation}\label{app:laptop-data-topsis}

Given the converted decision matrix of \Cref{tab:decision-matrix-converted} and the AHP
weight vector of \Cref{tab:ahp-weights}, the seven steps of \Cref{subsec:topsis-procedure}
are carried out as follows.

\paragraph{Step 2 --- Vector normalisation.}
Following \Cref{eq:norm}, each column of \Cref{tab:decision-matrix-converted} is divided by
the Euclidean norm of that column across the twelve candidates. \Cref{tab:topsis-norms}
reports the seven column norms; \Cref{tab:topsis-normalised} reports the resulting
normalised matrix $r_{ij}$.

\paragraph{Step 4 --- Ideal solutions.}
Following \Cref{eq:pis-nis}, for each of the seven columns of \Cref{tab:topsis-weighted},
the positive ideal solution $A^{+}$ takes the maximum value for a benefit criterion (CPU,
RAM, SSD, Brand, Battery) and the minimum for a cost criterion (Price, Weight); $A^{-}$
takes the opposite extreme in each case. \Cref{tab:topsis-ideal} reports both vectors.

\paragraph{Steps 5--7 --- Separation measures, closeness coefficient, and ranking.}
Following \Cref{eq:sep}, for each candidate, $S_i^{+}$ and $S_i^{-}$ are the Euclidean
distance from its row of \Cref{tab:topsis-weighted} to $A^{+}$ and $A^{-}$ respectively.
\Cref{tab:topsis-separation} reports both distances for all twelve candidates. Substituting
$S_i^{+}$ and $S_i^{-}$ into \Cref{eq:closeness} gives the closeness coefficient $C_i$ for
each candidate; the resulting values and final ranking are reported in
\Cref{tab:topsis-ranking} of \Cref{sec:ranking}.
