\begin{abstract}
\noindent
Laptop-selection studies consistently rank price, processing capability, and battery life
among the criteria that matter most to buyers, yet the priorities they report are drawn
from products, populations, and infrastructure conditions that do not automatically hold
once the buyer, the catalogue, and the country all change. This study addresses that gap
by developing and applying a three-stage decision model for office workers in Vietnam that
combines the Analytic Hierarchy Process (AHP) with the Technique for Order of Preference
by Similarity to Ideal Solution (TOPSIS). Candidate criteria were first synthesised from
prior studies and refined through semi-structured interviews with five experts positioned
across the procurement process, then screened through a survey of 84 respondents,
retaining seven criteria: price, processor, memory, storage, brand, weight, and battery
life. AHP weights were subsequently derived from the representative expert's pairwise
comparisons and verified for consistency ($CR = 0.048$); price (0.349) and processor
capability (0.277) jointly account for 62.6\% of the total priority, whereas battery life,
the most highly rated criterion in the screening survey, receives the smallest weight, its
status shifting from a valued attribute to a threshold already satisfied once mains power
can be assumed. TOPSIS then ranked twelve laptop models drawn from seven professional
review outlets using market data; the Lenovo ThinkPad T14 Gen 6 (AMD) ranked first
($C_i = 0.6152$) without dominating any single criterion outright, instead combining a
below-median price with a leading brand-share position into the most balanced profile in
the set, while the cheapest and the most expensive candidates ranked seventh and last. By
separating which attributes are locally relevant from how much each one should weigh, the
model turns an implicit purchase decision into a reproducible and auditable procedure that
procurement teams can update as catalogues change.

\vspace{1em}
\noindent\textbf{Keywords:} Multi-Criteria Decision-Making; AHP; TOPSIS; laptop selection; office workers; Vietnam.
\end{abstract}
